> 「The three months of winter are the season of *bì cáng* (闭藏, closure and storage). Water freezes and the earth cracks. Do not disturb the Yang. Retire early and rise late, and you must wait for the sunlight.」
>
> ——《黄帝内经·素问·四气调神大论》(*Huángdì Nèijīng*, *Sùwèn*, *Sìqì Tiáoshén Dàlùn*)

The "three months of winter" (冬三月) are the six solar terms of **Lìdōng (立冬, Beginning of Winter), Xiǎoxuě (小雪, Minor Snow), Dàxuě (大雪, Major Snow), Dōngzhì (冬至, Winter Solstice), Xiǎohán (小寒, Minor Cold) and Dàhán (大寒, Major Cold)**.
Heaven and Earth close and store; water freezes and the earth cracks; the Kidney of the human body, likewise, **stores the Essence (藏精, cáng jīng) and receives the Qi (纳气, nà qì)**.

> 「**The Kidney is the office of *zuò qiáng* (作强, doing-the-strenuous); from it issue the *jì qiǎo* (伎巧, skill and cleverness).**」

The Kidney is **"the root of the pre-Heaven" (先天之本, xiāntiān zhī běn)**, and it stores the Essence, governs water, governs the reception of Qi, governs the bones and produces the Marrow.
**"If in winter one does not store the Essence, in spring one will surely fall ill of warm disease" (冬不藏精，春必病温)** —
mistakes in winter's health preservation will be "settled" in spring, in the form of various illnesses.

## I. The Four Major Functions of the Kidney

### 1. Storing the Essence (藏精)

The Kidney stores the **congenital Essence (先天之精, xiāntiān zhī jīng, inherited from one's parents)** and the **acquired Essence (后天之精, hòutiān zhī jīng, from the transformation and transportation of the Spleen and Stomach)**.
The congenital Essence is the "**root of life (生命之根)**", and it determines a person's growth, development, aging, and lifespan.

> 「**The Essence is the root of the body; therefore those who store the Essence do not fall ill of warm disease in spring.**」

### 2. Governing Water (主水)

The Kidney is **the master switch of water metabolism (水液代谢的总开关)**.
The *qì huà* (气化) action of Kidney Yang causes the water to be steamed, transported, distributed, and excreted.
When Kidney Yang is deficient, the water stagnates and pools, giving rise to **edema (水肿), frequent night urination (夜尿多), and *tán yǐn* (痰饮, phlegm-fluid)**.

### 3. Governing the Reception of Qi (主纳气)

Although respiration is governed by the Lung, **the inhaled breath must be drawn down and received by the Kidney**.
> 「**The Lung governs exhalation; the Kidney governs the reception of Qi.**」

When the Kidney is deficient and cannot receive Qi, there is **more breathing out than in, and panting (呼多吸少、气喘)** — many elderly cases of chronic bronchitis and asthma have their root in the Kidney.

### 4. Governing the Bones, Producing the Marrow, with its Flourishing in the Hair (主骨生髓，其华在发)

- **Bones (骨)**: the Kidney stores the Essence, the Essence produces Marrow, the Marrow nourishes the bones. **Kidney deficiency brings soreness of the lower back and knees, and osteoporosis (腰膝酸软、骨质疏松)**.
- **Marrow (髓)**: the brain is the sea of Marrow. **Kidney deficiency brings poor memory, dizziness and tinnitus (记忆力下降、头晕耳鸣)**.
- **Hair (发)**: the hair is the surplus of blood, **but its root is in the Kidney**. **Kidney deficiency brings premature greying and hair loss (白发早生、脱发)**.
- **Ears (耳)**: the Kidney opens into the ears. **Kidney deficiency brings tinnitus, deafness, and decline of hearing (耳鸣耳聋、听力下降)**.

## II. Five Wisdoms for Nourishing the Kidney in Winter

### ❄ Wisdom One: Daily Routine — Retire Early, Rise Late, and Wait for Sunlight

The *Nèijīng* (《内经》, Inner Canon) is explicit: in winter one should **"retire early and rise late" (早卧晚起)**.

- **Early to bed (早卧)**: fall asleep in the *hài* hour (亥时, 21:00–23:00), **to nurture the Kidney and store the Essence (养肾藏精)**
- **Late to rise (晚起)**: rise only after sunlight (after **sunrise**), **to avoid the bitter cold**
- **Leave the cold and seek warmth (去寒就温)**: keep warm and avoid the cold, **especially the lower back, the feet, and the head**
- **Do not disturb the Yang (无扰乎阳)**: do not sweat heavily, do not labor to excess, do not rage, do not shout at the top of your voice

**Key areas to keep warm**:

- **Lower back (腰)**: the lower back is the residence of the Kidney, **in winter one must warm the lower back**
- **Feet (足)**: the feet are farthest from the heart and most easily invaded by cold, **wear thick socks**
- **Head (头)**: the head is the meeting-place of all the Yang, **"cold begins at the head"** — wear a hat when you go out
- **Back (背)**: the back is Yang, **"cold enters through the back"** — warm the back before sleep

### 🥘 Wisdom Two: Diet — Warming and Supplementing; Salty Enters the Kidney

> 「**The qi of winter is cold; one should eat *shǔ* (黍, glutinous millet) to use its heat to treat the cold.**」

In winter, **warmth and heat (温热) are the essentials of diet**, and one should eat little that is cold or cool.

**Foods suitable in winter**:

| Food | Channel Entry | Effect |
|------|------|------|
| Mutton (羊肉) | Spleen, Kidney | Warms the center and supplements deficiency, tonifies the Kidney and strengthens Yang |
| Beef (牛肉) | Spleen, Stomach | Tonifies the Spleen and Stomach, benefits Qi and blood |
| Walnut (核桃) | Kidney, Lung | Tonifies the Kidney and warms the Lung, moistens the Intestines and promotes bowel movement |
| Chestnut (栗子) | Spleen, Kidney | Tonifies the Kidney and strengthens the bones, strengthens the Spleen and nourishes the Stomach |
| Black sesame (黑芝麻) | Liver, Kidney | Tonifies the Liver and Kidney, benefits the Essence and blood, moistens Intestinal dryness |
| Black soybean (黑豆) | Spleen, Kidney | Tonifies the Kidney and benefits Yin, strengthens the Spleen and drains dampness |
| Black rice (黑米) | Spleen, Stomach | Tonifies the Kidney and strengthens the Spleen, nourishes Yin and enriches blood |
| Mulberry (桑葚) | Heart, Liver, Kidney | Nourishes Yin and supplements blood, generates fluids and moistens dryness |
| Longan (桂圆) | Heart, Spleen | Tonifies the Heart and benefits the Spleen, nourishes blood and quiets the spirit |
| Danggui (当归) | Liver, Heart, Spleen | Supplements blood and invigorates blood, regulates menstruation and relieves pain |

**The "Five Blacks Nourish the Kidney" (五黑养肾)**: black rice, black soybean, black sesame, black jujube, and mulberry.
To cook congee, paste or cake from these five blacks throughout winter is a **warming and moistening, top-grade Kidney tonic**.

**What to eat less of in winter**:

- ❌ Raw, cold, and cool foods (ice-cream, sashimi, cold-natured fruits)
- ❌ Sticky and hard-to-digest foods (glutinous rice balls, New Year cake, fatty meat)
- ❌ Spicy and over-dry foods (chili, strong liquor, barbecue)
- ❌ Excessive amounts of out-of-season fruits and vegetables

**Recommended around the Winter Solstice — Dāngguī Shēngjiāng Yángròu Tāng (当归生姜羊肉汤, Angelica–Ginger–Mutton Soup)**:
> 500 g mutton + 30 g Dāngguī (当归, Angelica sinensis) + 50 g fresh ginger + 5 jujubes, stew for 2 hours.

This is the **warming-Yang and supplementing-deficiency famous formula (温阳补虚名方)** of the medical sage Zhāng Zhòngjǐng (张仲景), originally designed to treat "**abdominal pain from cold *shàn* (寒疝, cold hernia), and pain in the chest and hypochondrium with internal urgency (胁痛里急)**",
and is also **the first home formula (家常第一方) for winter Kidney nourishment**.

### 🛌 Wisdom Three: Bedroom Conduct — Storing the Essence in Winter, No Warm Disease in Spring

> 「**If in winter one does not store the Essence, in spring one will surely fall ill of warm disease.**」

Winter is the season of **"closure and storage" (闭藏)**, and **the consumption of Kidney Essence should be especially restrained**.

**Principles of winter bedroom conduct**:

- ✅ **Moderation (适度)** — varies by individual, gauged by no fatigue the next day
- ✅ **Warmth (温暖)** — avoid wind and cold, **do not engage in intimacy while exposed to wind and cold**
- ✅ **Stillness (安静)** — keeping the spirit turned inward, not chasing after pleasures
- ❌ **After drinking (酒后)** — "**drunk one enters the bedroom, and through desire exhausts one's Essence (醉以入房，以欲竭其精)**", the greatest harm to the Kidney
- ❌ **At the great cold or great heat (大寒大热)** — great cold (around the Winter Solstice) and great heat (in summer) both call for restraint
- ❌ **After exhaustion (劳倦后)** — "**exhaustion depletes Qi (劳则气耗)**", intimacy after exhaustion most harms the body

> 「**At the age of forty, Yin qi is already half of what it was; one's daily vigor declines.**」——《素问·阴阳应象大论》(*Sùwèn*, *Yīnyáng Yīngxiàng Dàlùn*)

After forty, **the Kidney qi gradually declines**, and bedroom conduct should be **done in accordance with one's strength, erring on the side of less rather than more**.

### ☯ Wisdom Four: The Meridians — Moxibustion on Guānyuán; Rub and Knead Shènshù

#### 1. Guānyuán (CV-4, 关元穴, **the most Kidney-nourishing**)

- **Location**: 3 *cùn* directly below the navel
- **Function**: **cultivates the original and consolidates the root, supplements and benefits the lower burner (培元固本、补益下焦)**
- **Moxibustion method**: around the Winter Solstice, apply moxibustion 9 days in a row, 15–20 minutes per day
- **Special recommendation**: moxibustion on Guānyuán at the Winter Solstice is best able to **stimulate the pre-Heaven original Yang (先天元阳)**

#### 2. Shènshù (BL-23, 肾俞穴)

- **Location**: in the lower back, below the spinous process of the 2nd lumbar vertebra, 1.5 *cùn* lateral
- **Function**: **benefits the Kidney and assists Yang, receives Qi and governs water (益肾助阳、纳气利水)**
- **Moxibustion method**: gentle moxibustion 15–20 minutes
- **Companion practice**: rub the hands warm, then rub and knead Shènshù 100 times

#### 3. Mìngmén (GV-4, 命门穴)

- **Location**: in the lower back, in the depression below the spinous process of the 2nd lumbar vertebra
- **Function**: **warms the Kidney and strengthens Yang, strengthens the lower back and knees (温肾壮阳、强腰膝)**
- **Moxibustion method**: gentle moxibustion 15–20 minutes
- **Special recommendation**: around the Winter Solstice, moxibust **"Guānyuán + Mìngmén" (关元 + 命门) together** — **the warming-Kidney effect is strongest (温肾之功最强)**

#### 4. Yǒngquán (KD-1, 涌泉穴)

- **Location**: in the depression in the anterior one-third of the sole
- **Function**: **draws fire back to the origin, nourishes Yin and benefits the Kidney (引火归元、滋阴益肾)**
- **Moxibustion method**: gentle moxibustion 10–15 minutes
- **Special recommendation**: every night before bed, rub Yǒngquán 100 times — **calms sleep, nourishes the Kidney, descends fire (安眠、养肾、降火)**

#### 5. Yāoyǎn (Extra, 腰眼穴)

- **Location**: in the lower back, in the depression 3.5 *cùn* lateral to the spinous process of the 4th lumbar vertebra
- **Function**: **strengthens the lower back and the Kidney, quickens the blood and frees the connecting vessels (强腰健肾、活血通络)**
- **Method**: rub the hands warm, then press and knead Yāoyǎn 3–5 minutes

#### 6. Tàixī (KD-3, 太溪穴)

- **Location**: on the medial side of the foot, in the depression between the medial malleolus and the Achilles tendon
- **Function**: **nourishes Yin and benefits the Kidney, strengthens Yang and the lower back (滋阴益肾、壮阳强腰)**
- **Method**: press and knead with the thumb, 3 minutes per side, **until a sore and distended sensation is reached (至酸胀为度)**

### 🧘 Wisdom Five: *Dǎoyǐn* (导引) — Winter Kidney-Nourishing Exercises

#### 1. Standing Meditation (站桩)

Every morning on rising or before bed, stand in meditation for 10–20 minutes.
Both feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, **the head suspended from above, the chest held in and the back drawn up, the shoulders relaxed and the elbows dropped (虚领顶劲、含胸拔背、沉肩坠肘)**.
Standing meditation **cultivates the original and consolidates the root, warms and nourishes Kidney Yang (培元固本、温养肾阳)**, and is the best static exercise for winter.

#### 2. Tapping the Teeth and Swallowing the Saliva (叩齿咽津)

- On rising in the morning, first tap the teeth 36 times
- Then stir the tongue (move the tongue inside and outside the teeth) 36 times
- Then swish in the mouth (generating saliva) 36 times
- **Finally, swallow in 3 portions**, imagining the saliva being sent down to the *Dāntián* (丹田)

> 「**The Kidney governs saliva (肾主唾, shèn zhǔ tuò); the teeth are the surplus of the bones. Regularly tapping the teeth generates saliva, which nourishes the Kidney and consolidates the teeth.**」

#### 3. Anal-Lift Exercise (提肛功)

On inhalation, **slowly contract the perineum and anus**; on exhalation, slowly release them.
One lift and one release counts as one repetition; do 50–100 repetitions per day.
The **"Anal-Lift Exercise" (提肛功)** can **raise and lift Yang qi, consolidate the Essence and benefit the Kidney (升提阳气、固精益肾)**; over time, it benefits the **prostate, hemorrhoids, and uterine prolapse (前列腺、痔疮、子宫脱垂)**.

#### 4. Abdominal Rubbing (揉腹)

Before bed or on rising, lying on the back or seated, **with the navel as the center**, rub the abdomen **clockwise** for 100 circles, then **counterclockwise** for 100 circles.
**"The abdomen is the palace-city of the five viscera and six bowels" (腹为五脏六腑之宫城)**; abdominal rubbing **warms and nourishes Kidney Yang, strengthens the Spleen and harmonizes the Stomach (温养肾阳、健脾和胃)**.

#### 5. Rubbing the Yāoyǎn (搓腰眼)

Rub the hands warm, place them on the Yāoyǎn (next to the 4th lumbar vertebra), and **rub up and down** until the lower back feels warm (about 100–200 strokes).
**"The lower back is the residence of the Kidney" (腰为肾之府)**; rubbing the Yāoyǎn **warms the Kidney and strengthens Yang, quickens the collaterals and stops pain (温肾壮阳、活络止痛)**.

## III. The Ten Behaviors That Harm the Kidney in Winter

1. **Staying up late** (most injurious to Kidney Essence)
2. **Excessive bedroom activity** (most depleting of Kidney Essence)
3. **Long sitting without movement** (Kidney qi does not move)
4. **Abuse of medicines** (nephrotoxic medicines)
5. **High-salt diet** (injures Kidney Yin)
6. **Long-term holding of urine** (injures Kidney qi)
7. **Overexertion** (exhausts Kidney qi)
8. **Cold attacking the lower back and back** (cold enters from the back, injures Kidney Yang)
9. **Excessive fright** ("**fright injures the Kidney (恐伤肾)**")
10. **Abuse of tonics** (a deficient body cannot receive tonics, and the Kidney is hurt instead)

## IV. When to See a Doctor

If the following symptoms appear in winter, consult a doctor in good time:

- ⚠️ **Severe edema** (eyelids, lower limbs; the skin pits on pressure)
- ⚠️ **Abnormal urine volume** (significantly decreased or significantly increased)
- ⚠️ **Abnormal urine color** (dark tea color, washing-meat-water color, foamy)
- ⚠️ **Severe lower-back or abdominal pain** (possible renal colic)
- ⚠️ **Dizziness and visual disturbance** (renal hypertension)
- ⚠️ **Extreme fatigue** (Kidney Essence depletion)

What to store? Store **the Essence, store the Qi, store the Spirit, store the Yang**.
Store well a body's Essence, and in spring one can **live without illness**.

May you, through this winter —
