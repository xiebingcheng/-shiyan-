> 「**九月节，露气寒冷，将凝结也。**」
>
> "Mid-autumn — the dew turns cold and is about to congeal."
>
> — *Yuèlìng Qīshí'èr Hòu Jíjiě* (《月令七十二候集解》, Collection of Explanations of the Seventy-Two Pentads of the Monthly Ordinances)

Hánlù (寒露, Hánlù, Cold Dew) is **the fifth solar term of autumn**,
the **first of the twenty-four solar terms** to bear the character «**hán (寒, cold)**».
By this time, **summer heat has dispersed; autumn is in full swing; the dew bears chill**.

> 「**袅袅凉风动，凄凄寒露零。**」 ("Soft cool breezes stir; chilly cold dew falls.") — Bái Jūyì (白居易)

**Once Hánlù passes**,
**autumn** has truly **grown deep**.

## I. The Three Pentads of Hánlù

- **First pentad — The wild geese come as guests (鸿雁来宾):** the geese fly south, **the last flocks have all migrated**
- **Second pentad — Sparrows enter the great water and become clams (雀入大水为蛤):** sparrows vanish; **clams** appear in great numbers along the seashore
- **Third pentad — The chrysanthemum bears yellow flowers (菊有黄华):** **chrysanthemums** bloom; **Hánlù** is the very time for **viewing chrysanthemums**

The three pentads show **animals withdrawing and plants turning deep yellow** —
Heaven and Earth pass from **generation** into **contraction**.

## II. Three Characteristics of the Hánlù Season

### 1. Marked Drop in Temperature

At Hánlù, **temperatures are lower than at Báilù (白露, White Dew)**,
and **dew on the ground** is about to **freeze into frost**.
In the Yangtze basin, "**each autumn rain brings one chill more**";
in the north, "**autumn winds sweep the leaves**".

**«At Báilù, do not bare your body; at Hánlù, do not bare your feet»** —
after Hánlù, **the feet** must start being **kept warm**.

### 2. Air Becomes Dry

At Hánlù, **rainfall lessens**,
and **autumn dryness (秋燥, qiūzào)** grows ever more obvious —
**dry throat, dry nose, dry skin, hard stools**.

**«Dryness» is the master qi of autumn**;
**the Lung is a delicate organ and dreads dryness above all**.

### 3. Yang Qi Contracts

> 「**秋三月，此谓容平，天气以急，地气以明。**」 ("The three months of autumn: this is called *róng píng* (容平, the equilibrium of containment); the qi of Heaven becomes urgent, the qi of Earth becomes bright.")

Autumn belongs to Metal and corresponds to the Lung.
**The Lung governs diffusion and descending (主宣发肃降)** —
the focus of autumn yangsheng is on **«gathering» (收), «descending» (降), and «moistening» (润)**.

## III. Three Keys to Hánlù Health Preservation

### 🍂 Key One: Moisten the Lung and Nourish Yin

> 「**肺喜润而恶燥。**」 ("The Lung delights in moisture and detests dryness.")

Autumn dryness injures the Lung; **moistening the Lung** is the **first task**.

#### Diet for Moistening the Lung

- ✅ **Snow pear (雪梨, xuělí)** — moistens the Lung and generates fluids, the «**ancestor of the hundred fruits**»
- ✅ **Autumn pear paste (秋梨膏, qiūlígāo)** — enriches yin and moistens the Lung
- ✅ **Lily bulb (百合, bǎihé)** — moistens the Lung and stops cough, clears the Heart and quiets the spirit
- ✅ **White fungus (银耳, yín'ěr, Tremella)** — enriches yin and moistens the Lung, beautifies and combats aging
- ✅ **Màidōng (麦冬, Ophiopogon japonicus)** — nourishes yin and generates fluids, moistens the Lung and clears the Heart
- ✅ **Shāshēn (沙参, Glehnia / Adenophora)** — nourishes yin and clears the Lung, benefits the Stomach and generates fluids
- ✅ **Yùzhú (玉竹, Polygonatum odoratum)** — nourishes yin and moistens dryness, generates fluids and stops thirst
- ✅ **Shíhú (石斛, Dendrobium)** — enriches yin and clears heat, benefits the Stomach and generates fluids
- ✅ **Honey (蜂蜜, fēngmì)** — moistens the Lung and stops cough, moistens the intestines and unblocks the stool
- ✅ **Sesame (芝麻, zhīma)** — nourishes yin and moistens dryness, tonifies Liver and Kidney

**Recommended:**

- **Autumn Pear Paste (秋梨膏):** snow pear + màidōng + lily bulb + bèimǔ (贝母, fritillary) + honey, **simmered into a paste**, **the foremost lung-moistener of autumn**
- **White Fungus and Lily Bulb Soup (银耳百合汤):** white fungus + lily bulb + lotus seed + jujube, **nourishes yin and moistens the Lung**
- **Honey water:** 1-2 cups daily, **moistens intestines and stops cough**

#### Daily Routine for Moistening the Lung

- ✅ **Keep humidity in the dwelling** — humidifier or basin of water
- ✅ **Drink plenty of water** — **1500-2000 ml daily**
- ✅ **Warm, moist food** — congees, soups, broths
- ❌ **Dry-heat foods** — chilies, grills, strong liquor
- ❌ **Staying up late** — aggravates yin deficiency

#### Meridians for Moistening the Lung

- ✅ **Tàiyuān (太渊, LU-9)** — the source point of the Lung Meridian, **moistens the Lung and stops cough**
- ✅ **Fèishū (肺俞, BL-13)** — the back-shu point of the Lung, **regulates Lung qi**
- ✅ **Zhōngfǔ (中府, LU-1)** — the front-mu point of the Lung, **diffuses Lung qi and stops cough**
- ✅ **Lièquē (列缺, LU-7)** — the network-luo point of the Lung Meridian, **stops cough and calms wheeze**

### 🍁 Key Two: Nourish Yin and Prevent Dryness

> 「**春夏养阳，秋冬养阴。**」 ("Spring and summer nourish yang; autumn and winter nourish yin.")

Autumn governs gathering; nourishing yin is paramount.

#### Yin-Enriching Foods

- ✅ **Soft-shelled turtle (甲鱼, jiǎyú)** — enriches yin and cools blood
- ✅ **Duck meat (鸭肉, yāròu)** — enriches yin and nourishes the Stomach, disinhibits water and reduces swelling
- ✅ **Sea cucumber (海参, hǎishēn)** — enriches yin and tonifies the Kidneys
- ✅ **Sesame** — enriches yin and moistens dryness
- ✅ **Glutinous rice (糯米, nuòmǐ)** — enriches yin and tonifies qi
- ✅ **Mulberry (桑葚, sāngshèn)** — enriches yin and tonifies blood
- ✅ **Donkey-hide gelatin (阿胶, ējiāo)** — enriches yin and tonifies blood

#### Yin-Nourishing Daily Routine

- ✅ **Early to bed** — sleep by the *hài* hour (亥时, 21:00-23:00)
- ✅ **More sleep** — **one hour more** than in summer
- ✅ **Calm emotions** — "**make the will quiet, to soften autumn's severity**" (使志安宁，以缓秋刑)
- ❌ **Staying up late** — most damaging to yin
- ❌ **Strenuous exercise** — profuse sweat injures yin
- ❌ **Great anger** — "**anger injures the Liver; when Liver yin is injured, Lung yin is injured too**" (怒伤肝，肝阴伤则肺阴亦伤)

### 🍂 Key Three: Keep Warm and Guard Against Cold

> 「**白露身不露，寒露脚不露。**」 ("At Báilù do not bare the body; at Hánlù do not bare the feet.")

After Hánlù, the **«feet»** must begin to be **kept warm**.

#### Key Areas to Keep Warm

- ✅ **Feet** — "**cold rises from the feet**", **thick socks are a must**
- ✅ **Lower back** — "**the lower back is the seat of the Kidneys**"; keep it warm
- ✅ **Navel** — "**Shénquè (神阙, CV-8)**", **must not catch cold**
- ✅ **Head** — "**the head is the meeting of all yang**"; wear a hat outdoors

#### Dressing Principles

- ✅ **«Onion-layer» dressing** — many thin layers, **add or remove as needed**
- ❌ **«Autumn freezing» must be scientific** — "**autumn freezing**" means **moderate cold exposure**, **not** suffering chill
- ❌ **Ankles** — **must not be exposed** (**cold enters at the ankles**)
- ❌ **Navel** — **must not be exposed** (**Shénquè** dreads cold most)

## IV. Hánlù Seasonal Fruits and Vegetables

### 1. Snow Pear

> 「**润肺凉心，消痰降火。**」 ("Moistens the Lung and cools the Heart, dissolves phlegm and descends fire.")

- **Nature and flavor:** sweet, slightly sour, cool
- **Effects:** **moistens the Lung and generates fluids, clears heat and transforms phlegm**
- **Preparations:** **eaten raw, steamed, boiled in water, made into autumn pear paste**

**Rock Sugar Steamed Pear** — a seasonal food therapy for Hánlù:

- 1 snow pear, **core removed**
- Add **10 g rock sugar + 3 g chuānbèi (川贝, Sichuan fritillary) powder**
- **Steam for 30 minutes** in a double boiler
- Drink the broth, eat the pear — **moistens the Lung and stops cough**

### 2. Persimmon

> 「**柿，润肺止咳，清热生津。**」 ("Persimmon: moistens the Lung and stops cough, clears heat and generates fluids.")

- **Nature and flavor:** sweet, astringent, cool
- **Effects:** **moistens the Lung and generates fluids, clears heat and stops thirst**
- **Cautions:** **not on an empty stomach**, **not eaten with seafood**, **not in excess** (**contains tannic acid**)

### 3. Chrysanthemum

> 「**菊花，疏风清热、平肝明目。**」 ("Chrysanthemum: dispels wind and clears heat, calms the Liver and brightens the eyes.")

- **Nature and flavor:** sweet, bitter, slightly cool
- **Effects:** **dispels wind and clears heat, calms the Liver and brightens the eyes, moistens the Lung**
- **Preparations:** **chrysanthemum tea, chrysanthemum wine, chrysanthemum cake**

**Chrysanthemum Tea:**

- **5 chrysanthemum flowers + 10 gǒuqǐ (枸杞)** — nourishes the Liver and brightens the eyes
- **5 chrysanthemums + 10 g hawthorn (山楂, shānzhā)** — lowers blood pressure and reduces fat
- **5 chrysanthemums + 1/4 luóhàn guǒ (罗汉果, monk fruit)** — moistens the Lung and stops cough

### 4. Chestnut

> 「**栗，肾之果也，肾病宜食之。**」 ("Chestnut, the fruit of the Kidney; suitable for those with Kidney disease.")

- **Nature and flavor:** sweet, warm
- **Effects:** **tonifies the Kidneys and strengthens the bones, fortifies the Spleen and nourishes the Stomach, quickens blood and stops bleeding**
- **Preparations:** **sugar-roasted chestnuts, chestnut-braised chicken, chestnut congee**

### 5. Lotus Rhizome

> 「**藕，凉血散瘀、止渴除烦。**」 ("Lotus rhizome: cools blood and dispels stasis, stops thirst and dispels vexation.")

- **Nature and flavor:** sweet, cold
- **Effects:** **clears heat and generates fluids, cools blood and dispels stasis, fortifies the Spleen and opens the appetite**
- **Preparations:** **cold-tossed, stir-fried, in soup, as lotus root powder**

## V. Hánlù Exercise — «Climb High and Gaze Far»

> 「**重阳登高**」 ("Climbing high on the Double Ninth"), is precisely the Hánlù season.

Hánlù falls right around the **Double Ninth Festival (重阳, Chóngyáng)**,
when **climbing heights**, **viewing chrysanthemums**, and **gazing far**
are the most fitting **outdoor activities** of the season.

### Recommended Exercise

- ✅ **Climbing heights** — Double Ninth tradition, **strengthens the heart and lungs, exercises the legs and feet**
- ✅ **Jogging** — early morning or evening
- ✅ **Tai Chi (太极拳)** — combines movement and stillness
- ✅ **Eight Pieces of Brocade (八段锦)** — harmonizes qi and blood
- ✅ **Strolling** — "**a hundred steps after meals**"
- ❌ **Sweat-soaking exercise** — "**sweat is the fluid of the Heart**"

### Cautions for Exercise

- ✅ **After sunrise** for exercise
- ✅ **Warm up** before exercise
- ✅ **Add clothes** after exercise
- ❌ **Empty stomach** exercise
- ❌ **Profuse sweat** injures yin
- ❌ **Night-time** exercise

## VI. Hánlù Emotions — «Make the Will Quiet»

> 「**秋三月……使志安宁，以缓秋刑。**」 ("The three months of autumn… make the will quiet, to soften autumn's severity.")

Autumn's **«withering»** qi is at its strongest;
**emotions** easily turn **low, depressed, irritable**.

### Regulating Emotions

- ✅ **Climb heights and gaze far** — opens the heart and chest
- ✅ **View chrysanthemums** — cultivates the emotions
- ✅ **Listen to *shāng* and *jué* notes (商音, 角音)** — shāng moistens the Lung, jué courses the Liver
- ✅ **Get more sun** — sunlight suppresses melatonin, **prevents autumn depression**
- ✅ **Keep smiling** — "**a smile takes ten years off**"
- ❌ **Stay alone too long** — **solitude breeds depression**
- ❌ **Watch tragedies** — **lamenting autumn** injures the Lung

### Three Ways to Prevent Autumn Depression

1. **More sun** — sunlight is the best «antidepressant»
2. **More exercise** — exercise releases endorphins
3. **More communication** — confiding is the best release

## VII. Hánlù Daily Routine

### 1. «At Hánlù, Do Not Bare the Feet»

- After Hánlù, **no more barefoot** in sandals
- **Must wear thick or cotton socks**
- Cold rises from the feet; the **feet** above all need **warmth**

### 2. «In Autumn's Three Months, Early to Bed and Early to Rise, Up with the Rooster»

- **Early to bed:** sleep before the *hài* hour (21:00-23:00)
- **Early to rise:** rise **after sunrise**
- **Up with the rooster:** **rise at the cock's crow** (**5:00-7:00**)

### 3. «Guard Against Autumn Dryness»

- **Drink plenty of water** (warm water is best)
- **More congees** (yam congee, lily bulb congee, sesame congee)
- **Humidify the dwelling** (humidifier, basin of water)
- **Apply moisturizer** (to protect the skin)

## Conclusion

> 「**袅袅凉风动，凄凄寒露零。**」 ("Soft cool breezes stir; chilly cold dew falls.")

Hánlù is **the depths of autumn**,
**summer's farewell**,
**winter's prelude**.

Seize this **critical window for moistening the Lung and nourishing yin** —

- Eat more **snow pear, lily bulb, white fungus**
- Drink more **autumn pear paste, honey water**
- Press more often **Tàiyuān (LU-9), Fèishū (BL-13)**
- More **climbing, viewing chrysanthemums, gazing far**

Let this **deep autumn**
**soak through** our **whole-body yin**,
**gather in** our **whole-body yang**,
in preparation for the **«storage» of winter**.

**After Hánlù comes Shuāngjiàng (霜降, Frost's Descent)**.
**A season of autumn gathering** begins at **Hánlù**.
