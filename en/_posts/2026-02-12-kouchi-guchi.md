> 「**齿为骨之余，肾主骨，叩齿则骨坚，骨坚则齿牢。**」
>
> "The teeth are the surplus of the bones; the kidneys govern the bones. When one taps the teeth, the bones grow firm; when the bones are firm, the teeth are secure."
>
> ——《养生类纂》(*Yǎngshēng Lèizuǎn*, Compendium of Life-Nourishing Categories), quoting《医说》(*Yīshuō*, Discourses on Medicine)

Tooth-tapping (叩齿, kòu chǐ) is a small practice of **gently striking the upper and lower teeth together**.
Its method is utterly simple, yet its effects are vast —
the Tang-dynasty famous doctor **Sun Simiao (孙思邈, Sūn Sīmiǎo)** wrote in *Qiānjǐn Yàofāng* (《千金要方》, Thousand-Gold Essential Formulas): 「**叩齿三十六下，令齿虫不生**」 ("Thirty-six taps of the teeth, and tooth-worms shall not arise");
Song-dynasty **Su Dongpo (苏东坡, Sū Dōngpō)** also persisted in teeth-tapping, and "**to this day his face is like a child's, his ears keen and his eyes bright**";
**Emperor Qianlong (乾隆, Qiánlóng)** lived to 89 years of age, owing much to the practice of teeth-tapping.

Each day, three to five minutes of this practice will **firm the teeth, nourish the kidneys, fortify the spleen, beautify the complexion, sharpen the ears, and brighten the eyes** —
why not do it?

## I. The Origin of Teeth-Tapping

### Classical Records

- 《**Yǎngshēng Lèizuǎn**》(《养生类纂》, Compendium of Life-Nourishing Categories), quoting《*Yīshuō*》(《医说》): 「**叩齿则骨坚，骨坚则齿牢**」 ("Tap the teeth, and the bones grow firm; the bones firm, and the teeth are secure")
- 《***Qiānjǐn Yàofāng***》(《千金要方》, Thousand-Gold Essential Formulas): 「**每晨起，以盐末揩齿，叩齿百遍**」 ("Each morning on rising, rub the teeth with salt, and tap them a hundred rounds")
- 《***Yǎngshēng Bājiān***》(《养生八笺》, Eight Tablets on Life-Nourishment): 「**叩齿之法，左左相叩，名曰叩天钟**」 ("The method of teeth-tapping, left striking left, is called striking the Heavenly Bell")
- 《***Huángtíng Nèijǐng Jīng***》(《黄庭内景经》, *Huángtíng Nèijǐng*, Scripture of the Yellow Court's Interior View): 「**叩齿之法，名曰打天钟**」 ("The method of teeth-tapping is called striking the Heavenly Bell")

### Transmission by Famous Masters

- **Sun Simiao (孙思邈)** — Tang-dynasty medical sage, lived 101 years, benefited by the teeth-tapping practice
- **Su Dongpo (苏东坡)** — Northern Song literary giant, **tapped his teeth without fail each day**
- **Qianlong (乾隆)** — Qing-dynasty long-lived emperor, 89 years old, **tapped his teeth a hundred times on rising**
- **Wu Zetian (武则天)** — Tang-dynasty empress, **tapped her teeth to preserve her beauty**

## II. The Principles of Teeth-Tapping

### 1. The TCM Mechanism

> 「**肾藏精，主骨生髓，齿为骨之余。**」 ("The kidneys store *jīng* (精, essence), govern the bones and engender marrow; the teeth are the surplus of the bones.")

- **Teeth are the surplus of the bones (齿为骨之余, chǐ wéi gǔ zhī yú):** the teeth are an extension of the skeleton; **the kidneys govern the bones (肾主骨, shèn zhǔ gǔ)**, so the teeth are closely related to the kidneys.
- **Teeth-tapping activates kidney qi (肾气, shènqì):** it allows the **kidney qi to be abundant, the bones to be firm, and the teeth to be solid**.
- **Teeth-tapping fortifies the spleen (脾, pí):** **the teeth are the gateway (齿为门户)**, so tapping promotes **digestion and fortifies the spleen and stomach (脾胃, píwèi)**.
- **Teeth-tapping generates fluids (生津, shēng jīn):** when tapping, the mouth **produces fluids (口中生津)**, that is, saliva; saliva is **"golden fluid and jade liquid" (金津玉液)**.

### 2. Modern Research

Modern medicine has likewise confirmed the benefits of teeth-tapping:

- ✅ **Firms the teeth:** tapping promotes **blood circulation in the periodontal tissues (牙周血液循环)**, enhancing periodontal health.
- ✅ **Prevents cavities (龋齿, qǔchǐ):** tapping cleanses **the tooth surface (牙齿表面)**.
- ✅ **Prevents gingival recession (牙龈萎缩):** tapping **slows resorption of the alveolar bone (延缓牙槽骨吸收)**.
- ✅ **Promotes digestion:** tapping **increases saliva secretion (唾液分泌增加)**; the **amylase (淀粉酶)** in saliva aids digestion.
- ✅ **Strengthens immunity:** the **lysozyme (溶菌酶)** and **immunoglobulins (免疫球蛋白)** in saliva are antibacterial.
- ✅ **Slows aging:** tapping promotes **blood circulation in the face (面部血液循环)**, leaving one with **a childlike complexion (面如童颜)**.

## III. The Specific Method of Teeth-Tapping

### The "Three Tappings" Method (三叩功法)

#### 1. Tapping the Incisors (叩门牙)

- **Method:** gently tap the upper and lower incisors against each other
- **Count:** **36 times**
- **Force:** **from light to heavy, light before heavy**

#### 2. Tapping the Canines (叩犬牙)

- **Method:** gently tap the upper and lower canines against each other
- **Count:** **36 times**
- **Force:** **moderate**

#### 3. Tapping the Molars (叩臼齿)

- **Method:** gently tap the upper and lower molars (large teeth) against each other
- **Count:** **36 times**
- **Force:** **somewhat heavier, to a vibrating sensation**

### The "Three Tappings" Total 108

> 「**三十六下为一节，三节为一遍**」 ("Thirty-six taps form one section; three sections form one round")

### The Complete Practice: "Teeth-Tapping and Swallowing Saliva" (叩齿吞津)

#### Step One: Teeth-Tapping

- Relax the whole body, **keep the lips lightly closed (口唇轻闭)**
- **Gently tap** the upper and lower teeth together
- **First tap the incisors 36 times**
- **Then tap the canines 36 times**
- **Finally tap the molars 36 times**
- Total: **108 times**

#### Step Two: Stirring the Tongue (搅舌)

- After teeth-tapping, **the tongue (舌头)** stirs **inside and outside the teeth**
- **Outside first, then inside**, **clockwise (顺时针) 18 times**, **counter-clockwise (逆时针) 18 times**
- Total: **36 times**

#### Step Three: Gurgling the Mouth (鼓漱)

- After stirring the tongue, **the mouth has produced fluids (口中已生津液)**
- **Close the mouth and gurgle** 36 times
- Let the fluids **fully contact the gums (齿龈) and oral mucosa (口腔黏膜)**

#### Step Four: Swallowing the Saliva (咽津)

- **In three mouthfuls**, slowly swallow the fluids in the mouth
- Imagine the fluids **"gurgling" (汩汩)** down to the **"dāntián" (丹田)** (3 *cùn* (寸) below the navel)
- Best is to **make a gurgling sound (咕咕有声)** as you swallow

### Duration of the Practice

The whole "**teeth-tapping and swallowing saliva (叩齿吞津)**" practice takes about **3-5 minutes**.

## IV. The Best Times for Teeth-Tapping

### 🌅 1. Teeth-Tapping at Dawn (**Most Important**)

- **Timing:** **after getting up, before washing the face**
- **Reason:** after a night's rest, **the fluids in the mouth have gathered and not yet dispersed**; tapping them causes **the greatest generation of fluids (生津最旺)**
- **Effects:** **fortifies the spleen and opens the stomach, refreshes the spirit, brightens the eyes**
- **Companion practice:** **drinking a cup of warm water (温水)** after tapping is even better

### 🌙 2. Teeth-Tapping Before Sleep

- **Timing:** **before sleep, after brushing the teeth**
- **Reason:** **firms the teeth and strengthens the bones, calms the heart and quietens the spirit**
- **Effects:** **aids sleep, firms the teeth, tonifies the kidneys**
- **Companion practice:** **rub the Yǒngquán point (涌泉, KD-1) 100 times** after tapping, to **return fire to its source (引火归元)**

### 🍽 3. Teeth-Tapping After Meals

- **Timing:** **30 minutes after a meal** (do not tap right after eating)
- **Reason:** **fortifies the spleen and aids digestion**
- **Effects:** **promotes digestion, firms the teeth**
- **Caveat:** when **food residue is present** do not tap; rinse the mouth first

### 💪 4. Teeth-Tapping When Tired

- **Timing:** **after long sitting, when fatigued**
- **Effects:** **refreshes the mind and restores energy**

## VI. Precautions for Teeth-Tapping

### ✅ 1. Force Should Be Moderate

- **From light to heavy, light before heavy**
- **Do not use excessive force**, lest you **damage the tooth enamel (牙釉质)**
- Those with healthy teeth may tap **somewhat harder**
- Those with loose teeth should tap **very lightly**

### ✅ 2. Speed Should Be Even

- **1-2 taps per second**
- **Not too fast**, lest the teeth collide too frequently
- **Not too slow**, lest fluid production be insufficient

### ✅ 3. Count Should Be Sufficient

- **Begin with 36 taps each time**
- **108 taps** is best
- **Do not** stop after only a few

### ✅ 4. Persevere

- **1-2 times daily** (on rising + before sleep)
- **Persist for 3 months**, and effects will appear
- **Persist for 1 year**, and teeth will be firm and a childlike complexion restored

### ❌ 5. Use with Care When the Teeth Are Diseased

- During **acute flare-ups of periodontitis (牙周炎急性发作)** or **severe toothache (牙齿剧痛)**, **suspend teeth-tapping**
- With **dental trauma (牙齿外伤)**, tapping is **forbidden**
- Those with **severe cavities** should **treat the cavities first**, then tap

## VII. Combining Teeth-Tapping with Other Practices

### 1. Teeth-Tapping + Rubbing Yǒngquán (涌泉, KD-1)

- **On rising:** tap teeth → drink warm water → rub Yǒngquán (涌泉)
- **Before sleep:** tap teeth → rub Yǒngquán → rest
- 「**Teeth-tapping nourishes the kidneys + rubbing Yǒngquán returns fire**」 is **the best combination for sleep**

### 2. Teeth-Tapping + Rubbing the Ears

- Tap the teeth 36 times → rub the ears 36 times
- Ear-rubbing method: rub the palms of both hands until warm, cover both ears, **rub up and down** until **the ears feel hot**

### 3. Teeth-Tapping + Ming Tiangu (鸣天鼓, "Drum of Heaven")

- Tap the teeth 36 times → perform Míng Tiāngǔ 36 times
- Míng Tiāngǔ: cover both ears with the palms, **snap the index fingers against the back of the head** 36 times
- 「**Teeth-tapping firms the teeth + Míng Tiāngǔ sharpens the ears**」 — **firm teeth, keen ears**

### 4. Teeth-Tapping + Abdominal Rubbing

- On rising, tap teeth → drink warm water → rub the abdomen 100 circles
- 「**Teeth-tapping fortifies the spleen + abdominal rubbing moves the spleen**」 — **dual care for the spleen and stomach**
