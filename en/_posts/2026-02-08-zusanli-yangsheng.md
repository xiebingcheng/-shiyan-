> 「**If you wish for peace, keep Zúsānlǐ ever moxa-ed.**」
>
> ——Sūn Sīmiǎo, *Qiānjīn Yàofāng* (《千金要方》, Thousand Golden Prescriptions)

Zúsānlǐ is the **He-Sea point (合穴) of the Foot-Yangming Stomach Meridian**, and the **Lower He-Sea point (胃下合穴) of the Stomach**;
it is the **first of the TCM "Four General Command Points" (四总穴)**, and one of the **"Nine Acupuncture Points for Restoring Yang" (回阳九针穴)** —
it is the **foremost acupuncture point (第一要穴) for strengthening the body and prolonging life**.

The Tang-dynasty Sūn Sīmiǎo called it the **"Longevity Point" (长寿穴)**;
the Song-dynasty Zhāng Gǎo recorded in *Yīshuō* (《医说》, Discussions of Medicine): "**To needle and moxa this point lets the patient's Qi descend, and enables him to walk briskly**";
to this day, the Japanese folk tradition still practices **"Zúsānlǐ moxibustion" (三里灸)**, known as the **"Longevity Moxibustion" (长寿灸)**.

## I. Locating Zúsānlǐ (ST-36)

### Simple Locating Method

1. **Step 1**: bend the knee at 90°, place the palm on the kneecap
2. **Step 2**: with the **tip of the middle finger**, slide up along the lateral side of the tibia
3. **Step 3**: to the **most obvious depression on the lateral side below the knee** (point *Dúbí*, ST-35, the "calf's nose")
4. **Step 4**: measure **four finger-widths (tóngshēn cùn 3 cùn) downward** from *Dúbí*
5. **Step 5**: **one finger-width lateral to the tibia** — that is the point

> 「**Three *cùn* below the knee, on the lateral aspect of the tibia, between the two sinews; locate it with the foot raised.**」——《灵枢·本输》(*Língshū*, *Běnshū*)

### Precise Anatomical Location

- On the **anterolateral aspect of the lower leg**, 3 *cùn* below the lateral knee-eye (point *Dúbí*, ST-35)
- **One finger-width (middle finger) from the anterior crest of the tibia**
- Between the **tibialis anterior muscle** and the **extensor digitorum longus muscle**
- Innervated by the **lateral sural cutaneous nerve**, the **cutaneous branch of the saphenous nerve**, and the **deep peroneal nerve**; supplied by the **anterior tibial artery and vein**

## II. The Three Major Properties of Zúsānlǐ

### 1. He-Sea Point (合穴)

**"Where the channel enters is the He-Sea" (所入为合)** — Zúsānlǐ is the **He-Sea point of the Stomach Meridian**,
the place where the Qi of the Stomach Meridian is **most abundant and flourishing**,
and pressing, moxaing, or needling it is best able to **mobilize the Qi of the Stomach Meridian**.

### 2. Lower He-Sea Point of the Stomach (胃下合穴)

**"The He-Sea treats the hollow organs" (合治内腑)** — Zúsānlǐ is the place where the Qi of the Stomach hollow organ **descends and unites in the foot**,
so for all **diseases of the Stomach hollow organ** (stomach pain, vomiting, abdominal distension, indigestion),
Zúsānlǐ is the **first choice**.

### 3. Strong-Tonic Point (强壮要穴)

Zúsānlǐ is one of the **"Nine Acupuncture Points for Restoring Yang" (回阳九针穴)**,
and is able to **support the upright and cultivate the original, strengthen the Spleen and harmonize the Stomach, raise and lower the Qi mechanism, and free the connecting vessels (扶正培元、健脾和胃、升降气机、疏通经络)**.
In all cases of **deficiency, aging, chronic illness, or post-illness weakness**, Zúsānlǐ is a must-needle point.

## III. The Ten Major Functions of Zúsānlǐ

### 1. Strengthen the Spleen and Harmonize the Stomach (健脾和胃)

For stomach pain, abdominal distension, vomiting, hiccups, poor appetite, indigestion, **Zúsānlǐ is the first point**.

> Combined with **Zhōngwǎn (中脘, CV-12, Front-Mu point of the Stomach)** — for stomach pain and epigastric stuffiness
> Combined with **Nèiguān (内关, PC-6, Confluent point of the *Yīn Wéi* vessel)** — for vomiting and hiccups
> Combined with **Gōngsūn (公孙, SP-4, Confluent point of the *Chōng* vessel)** — for abdominal distension and diarrhea

### 2. Supplement Qi and Blood (补益气血)

Zúsānlǐ is the **representative point of the "Root of the Post-Heaven" (后天之本)**,
**"the Spleen and Stomach are the source of Qi and blood generation" (脾胃为气血生化之源)**,
and Zúsānlǐ can make **the Spleen and Stomach strong, Qi and blood flourishing**.

> Combined with **Sānyīnjiāo (三阴交, SP-6)** — strengthens the Spleen and nourishes blood
> Combined with **Qìhǎi (气海, CV-6)** — greatly supplements the original Qi
> Combined with **Xuèhǎi (血海, SP-10)** — supplements blood and regulates menstruation

### 3. Strengthen the Body (强壮身体)

> 「**The merit of Zúsānlǐ is known throughout the world,**」 — old verse on acupuncture

Zúsānlǐ can **enhance immunity, relieve fatigue, and slow aging**.
In Japanese folk tradition there is the practice of **"traveller's moxibustion on Zúsānlǐ" (旅行灸三里)** — before setting out on a long journey, moxa Zúsānlǐ, and one can **travel with light, brisk steps and abundant energy**.

### 4. Harmonize Qi and Blood (调和气血)

Zúsānlǐ can **regulate Qi, harmonize blood, support the upright, and expel the pathogenic**,
and exerts a bidirectional regulatory effect on **high blood pressure, low blood pressure, anemia, and palpitations**.

### 5. Dispel Dampness and Transform Phlegm (祛湿化痰)

Zúsānlǐ is an important point for **transforming phlegm-dampness**,
and has a regulating effect on **phlegm-dampness constitution, obesity, and fatty liver**.
> Combined with **Fēnglóng (丰隆, ST-40, the key point for transforming phlegm)** — transforms phlegm and dispels dampness
> Combined with **Yīnlíngquán (阴陵泉, SP-9, strengthens the Spleen and dispels dampness)** — strengthens the Spleen and transforms dampness

### 6. Free the Connecting Vessels and Stop Pain (通络止痛)

Zúsānlǐ has good effect on **flaccidity and obstruction of the lower limbs, knee-joint pain, and sciatica**.
> Combined with **Yánglíngquán (阳陵泉, GB-34, the Confluence point of the sinews)** — for knee-joint pain
> Combined with **Huántiào (环跳, GB-30)** — for sciatica

### 7. Descend Counterflow and Stop Vomiting (降逆止呕)

> 「**For abdominal troubles, leave it to Zúsānlǐ**」 — Song of the Four General Command Points

For all **abdominal complaints**, Zúsānlǐ can regulate them.
Especially for **Stomach Qi ascending counterflow** (vomiting, hiccups, belching), Zúsānlǐ's **descending-counterflow effect is the strongest**.

### 8. Quiet the Heart and Settle the Will (安神定志)

Zúsānlǐ can **calm the heart and quiet the spirit**, and has a regulating effect on **insomnia, vexation, and palpitations**.
> Combined with **Shénmén (神门, HT-7, Yuan-Source point of the Heart)** — for insomnia and profuse dreaming
> Combined with **Bǎihuì (百会, GV-20)** — lifts Yang, calms the heart and settles the will

### 9. Prevent Disease and Preserve Health (防病保健)

> 「**If the moxibustion on Zúsānlǐ is never stopped, all calamities and illnesses are stilled.**」 — Japanese folk saying

Zúsānlǐ's effects in **preventing disease and prolonging life** are recognized worldwide.
The Japanese doctor **Hara Shimentarō (原志免太郎)** and his family, for four generations, **moxaed Zúsānlǐ daily**, and all lived past the age of ninety.

### 10. Rescue from Collapse and Restore Yang (急救回阳)

Zúsānlǐ is one of the **"Nine Acupuncture Points for Restoring Yang" (回阳九针穴)**,
and for acute conditions of Yang Qi on the verge of collapse, such as **collapse, syncope, and shock**,
**heavy moxibustion on Zúsānlǐ** has the effect of restoring Yang and rescuing from counterflow.

## IV. Three Health-Maintenance Methods for Zúsānlǐ

### 1. Press-and-Knead Method (按揉法)

- **Technique**: use the **pad of the thumb** to press on Zúsānlǐ
- **Force**: **from light to heavy, to the degree of tolerance**
- **Duration**: 3–5 minutes per side, **to the point of a sore and distended sensation**
- **Timing**: best at **morning on rising, before bed, and 1 hour after meals**
- **Frequency**: **1–2 times per day**

**Key points**:

- Keep the nails trimmed, to avoid scratching the skin
- The pressing force should **penetrate, not just be a surface massage**
- Combine with **deep breathing**, for better effect
- Press one side, then the other — **3–5 minutes on each side**

### 2. Moxibustion Method (艾灸法) — **Most Recommended**

- **Method**: **mild moxibustion (温和灸)** or **ginger-separated moxibustion (隔姜灸)**
- **Duration**: **15–20 minutes per side**
- **Frequency**: **once daily or once every other day**
- **Course of treatment**: **10 sessions make one course**, rest 5–7 days, then continue

**Mild moxibustion (温和灸)**:

- Light the moxa stick, aim it at Zúsānlǐ
- **2–3 cm** from the skin
- Keep it **warm but not burning**
- Moxa until **the skin turns rosy (皮肤潮红)** — best

**Ginger-separated moxibustion (隔姜灸) (most supplementing)**:

- Cut a slice of ginger **0.3 cm thick**
- Prick several small holes in it
- Place it on Zúsānlǐ
- Place a moxa cone on the ginger
- When it burns out, replace; **5–9 cones per point (壮)**

> 「**If the moxibustion on Zúsānlǐ is never stopped, all calamities and illnesses are stilled.**」

**Special recommendation — "Zúsānlǐ moxibustion" for the four seasons**:

- **Spring**: nourish the Liver and Spleen (Zúsānlǐ + Tàichōng)
- **Summer**: nourish the Heart and Spleen (Zúsānlǐ + Nèiguān)
- **Autumn**: nourish the Lung and Spleen (Zúsānlǐ + Fèishù, BL-13)
- **Winter**: nourish the Kidney and Spleen (Zúsānlǐ + Shènshù, BL-23 + Guānyuán, CV-4)

### 3. Tapping Method (敲打法)

- **Technique**: make a **loose fist**, and tap Zúsānlǐ with **the ulnar (little-finger) side**
- **Force**: **moderate to light**, just enough to feel a vibration
- **Duration**: **100–200 strikes per side**
- **Frequency**: **1–2 times per day**

Suitable for: people with weak hands, who cannot press for long.

## V. Contraindications of Zúsānlǐ

### ❌ 1. Pregnant Women: Use with Care

Pregnant women **must not heavily moxa, must not strongly needle** Zúsānlǐ,
to avoid **excessive invigoration of blood and freeing of the connecting vessels**, which may disturb the fetal qi.
> 「**Pregnant women are forbidden to needle Hégǔ (LI-4) and Sānyīnjiāo (SP-6); pregnant women are forbidden to moxa Zúsānlǐ.**」

### ❌ 2. Excess and Heat Syndromes: Use with Care

Zúsānlǐ's **supplementing effect is the strongest**;
in **excess syndromes, heat syndromes, and when pathogenic Qi is abundant**, use with care,
to avoid **"closing the door to keep the bandit inside" (闭门留寇)**.

### ❌ 3. Local Skin Lesions: Moxibustion Forbidden

When the skin over Zúsānlǐ has **abrasion, ulceration, or sores**, moxibustion is forbidden.

### ❌ 4. Extreme Fatigue, Starvation or Satiation, After Drinking

At these times **Qi and blood are disordered**; heavy moxibustion or heavy pressing on Zúsānlǐ may **aggravate discomfort**.

### ❌ 5. Infants and Young Children

Infants and young children have **delicate skin and poor cooperation**; both pressing and moxibustion require **professional medical guidance**.

## Conclusion

> 「**Zúsānlǐ has the strengthening of Báizhú (白术), the scattering of Chuānxiōng (川芎),**」
> 「**the supplementing of Dāngguī (当归), the draining of Huánglián (黄连),**」
> 「**it can ascend and can descend, can drain and can supplement,**」
> 「**its function embraces all medicines — it is the foremost acupuncture point of the human body.**」

Zúsānlǐ is not a point for treating a single disease,
it is a point for treating **the "pathogenesis" of "deficiency" (治「虚弱」这个「病机」)**.
It is a point for treating **"daily wear and tear" (日常损耗)**.
It is a point for treating **"the passing of time" (时光流逝)**.

May you **press or moxa Zúsānlǐ for 15 minutes every day**,
**and see the Spleen and Stomach grow stronger, Qi and blood grow richer, the legs grow lighter, and the years grow longer**.
