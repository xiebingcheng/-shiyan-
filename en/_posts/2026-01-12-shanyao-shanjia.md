> 「Yam, also named *shǔyù* (薯蓣). Flavor sweet and warm. It governs injury to the center, supplements weakness and emaciation, removes the qi of cold and heat, supplements the center and boosts qi and strength, and increases the muscles. Long-term ingestion brightens the ears and eyes, lightens the body and abolishes hunger, and extends the years.」
>
> ——《神农本草经》(*Shénnóng Běncǎo Jīng*, Divine Farmer's Classic of Materia Medica)

The Chinese yam is of the **yam family (薯蓣科, shǔyù kē)**. Its name first appears in the *Shānhǎi Jīng* (《山海经》, Classic of Mountains and Seas), where it is called "*shǔyù*" (薯蓣);
Later, because Emperor Dàizōng of the Tang (唐代宗) bore the personal name "*Yù*" (豫), a taboo was issued and the name was changed to "*shǔyào*" (薯药); and later still, because Emperor Yīngzōng of the Song (宋英宗) bore the personal name "*Shǔ*" (曙), another taboo was issued and it was changed to "*shānyào*" (山药) — these two consecutive naming taboos gave it the beautiful epithet "the medicine of the mountains".

## I. Why the Chinese Yam Is a "Superior-Grade" Herb

Traditional Chinese Medicine divides medicinals into three grades: **superior-grade (上品) are non-toxic, govern the nurturing of destiny and correspondence with Heaven, and may be taken long-term**; middle-grade have slight toxicity and govern the nurturing of nature; inferior-grade have strong toxicity and govern the treatment of disease.
The Chinese yam sits at the **very head of the superior grade**, which means it is —

- ✅ **May be taken long-term** — suitable for everyday use
- ✅ **Mild in nature** — neither cold nor hot, suitable for young and old
- ✅ **Multi-effective** — tonifies multiple organs, treats multiple conditions
- ✅ **Non-toxic** — very few side effects

This kind of "gentle yet all-around" herb is **the first choice for ordinary households seeking health preservation**.

## II. The Four Natures, Flavors and Channel Entries of the Chinese Yam

- **Nature and flavor (性味)**: sweet, neutral (raw slightly cool, cooked slightly warm)
- **Channel entry (归经)**: enters the three meridians of **Spleen, Lung, and Kidney (脾、肺、肾)**
- **Functions (功效)**: **tonifies the Spleen and nourishes the Stomach, generates fluids and benefits the Lung, tonifies the Kidney and astringes the Essence (补脾养胃、生津益肺、补肾涩精)**

Please pay attention to the word "**astringe (涩)**" — the Chinese yam **tonifies the Kidney and secures through astringency**;
for men with seminal emissions and women with leukorrhea, it has a regulating effect; it tonifies without drying, astringes without stagnating.

## III. Huai Yam, Iron-Stick Yam, Common Yam — Which Is Best?

Three kinds of yam are common on the market:

| Variety | Place of Origin | Characteristics | Use |
|------|------|------|------|
| **Iron-stick yam (铁棍山药)** | Jiaozuo, Henan (ancient Huaiqing Prefecture) | Slender, dense hairs, fine-grained cut, abundant mucilage | **Best for medicinal use** |
| **Huai yam (淮山药)** | Jiangsu, Anhui (Huai River basin) | Stout, crisp, low in fiber | Both food and medicine |
| **Common table yam** | Various regions | Thick, watery, coarse-fibered | Mostly for cooking |

> The term "**Huai yam (怀山药)**" refers specifically to the **Iron-stick yam grown in the area of Wen County, Jiaozuo, Henan**, named for belonging in ancient times to Huaiqing Prefecture. Among Iron-stick yams, the **lú-soil Iron-stick (垆土铁棍)** is the finest, with firm texture and full flavor.

**The four-word rhyme for choosing a good yam**:

- **Look (看)**: rusty-red mottles on the surface, dense root-hairs
- **Weigh (掂)**: for the same size, the heavier the better (plenty of moisture, high density)
- **Snap (掰)**: snow-white cross-section, stringy mucilage, fine and grainless
- **Steam (蒸)**: after steaming, dry-floury and sweet, neither watery nor woody

## IV. Ten Home-Cooked Chinese Yam Recipes

### 1. Chinese Yam and Millet Porridge (Spleen–Stomach Weakness)

- **Method**: dice 50 g of Chinese yam; add 80 g of millet; cook together into porridge
- **Effect**: tonifies the Spleen, nourishes the Stomach, increases appetite
- **Indicated for**: Spleen–Stomach weakness, poor appetite, indigestion

### 2. Blueberry Chinese Yam (Child Anorexia)

- **Method**: steam the Chinese yam until soft, mash into purée, drizzle with blueberry sauce
- **Effect**: strengthens the Spleen, whets the appetite, attractive color
- **Indicated for**: picky eating, anorexic children

### 3. Chinese Yam and Pork-Rib Soup (Weakness After Illness)

- **Method**: 200 g Chinese yam + 500 g pork ribs + 5 jujubes, stew for 2 hours
- **Effect**: tonifies the Spleen, boosts qi, strengthens sinews and bones
- **Indicated for**: post-illness, post-surgery, post-partum weakness

### 4. Chinese Yam and *Qiànshí* Porridge (Spleen and Kidney Both Deficient)

- **Method**: 50 g Chinese yam + 30 g *qiànshí* (fox nut, Euryale ferox) + 80 g polished rice, cook into porridge
- **Effect**: **doubly tonifies the Spleen and Kidney**, secures the Essence and stops diarrhea
- **Indicated for**: soreness of the lower back and knees, frequent night urination, persistent diarrhea

### 5. Chinese Yam and Lotus Seed Soup (Restless Heart-Spirit)

- **Method**: 50 g Chinese yam + 30 g lotus seeds + appropriate rock sugar, stew until soft
- **Effect**: tonifies the Spleen, nourishes the Heart, quiets the spirit and aids sleep
- **Indicated for**: palpitations, insomnia, poor memory

### 6. Chinese Yam, Job's Tears and *Qiànshí* (Dampness-Heavy, Spleen-Deficient)

- **Method**: 50 g Chinese yam + 30 g dry-fried Job's tears (薏米, *yìmǐ*) + 20 g *qiànshí*, simmer in water as a tea
- **Effect**: strengthens the Spleen, drains dampness, secures the Kidney and stops diarrhea
- **Indicated for**: heavy dampness, white-greasy tongue coating, loose stools

### 7. *Bāsī* Chinese Yam — Caramelized Yam (Winter Nourishment)

- **Method**: cut the Chinese yam into irregular chunks, deep-fry until golden, coat in sugar syrup and pull into threads
- **Effect**: tonifies the Kidney, augments the Essence, warms the center and supplements deficiency
- **Indicated for**: winter health preservation

### 8. Chinese Yam Steamed Cake (Spleen-Strengthening Staple)

- **Method**: yam purée + glutinous rice flour + brown sugar, steam for 30 minutes
- **Effect**: tonifies the Spleen, stops diarrhea, secures the Kidney and augments the Essence
- **Indicated for**: Spleen-deficiency chronic diarrhea, infantile *gān jī* (malnutrition)

### 9. Chinese Yam and Chicken Stew (Qi and Blood Both Supplemented)

- **Method**: 300 g Chinese yam + half an old hen + 10 g *huángqí* (astragalus), stew for 2 hours
- **Effect**: supplements qi, nourishes blood, strengthens the Spleen and secures the Kidney
- **Indicated for**: qi and blood dual deficiency, fatigue and lack of strength

### 10. Chinese Yam and Jujube Paste (Daily Breakfast)

- **Method**: 100 g Chinese yam + 5 jujubes + 50 g polished rice, blend into a paste
- **Effect**: tonifies the Spleen, harmonizes the Stomach, nourishes blood and quiets the spirit
- **Indicated for**: the whole family

## V. Contraindications

Although the Chinese yam is mild, there are still contraindications:

- ❌ **Those with abundant dampness and center-fullness (湿盛中满)** should use with care — abdominal distension and greasy tongue coating, damp stagnation is aggravated
- ❌ **Those with constipation** should use sparingly — the Chinese yam is astringent; in constipated persons it may worsen the condition
- ❌ **Do not take together with alkaline medicines** — the yam contains amylase, which is destroyed by alkali
- ❌ **Do not eat large amounts raw** — raw yam contains small amounts of saponin, which in excess irritates the throat
- ❌ **Diabetics should take sparingly** — the starch content is high; quantity should be controlled

A very few people will experience **skin itching or rashes (皮肤瘙痒、红疹)** as an allergic reaction after eating Chinese yam, mostly from contact with the raw yam's mucilage;
handling with gloves or disposable plastic gloves will prevent this.

## VI. Chinese Yam Leaves and Chinese Yam Bulblets

**Chinese yam leaves (山药叶, shānyào yè)** (the tender leaves on the vine): can be stir-fried or made into soup, **slightly cool in nature**, clearing the Liver and brightening the eyes.
**Chinese yam bulblets (山药豆, shānyào dòu)** (the small tubers that grow on the yam vine, also called *líng yú zǐ* 零余子): neutral in nature and sweet in flavor, **tonify the Lung and benefit the Kidney, astringe the Essence and stop leukorrhea**.
A Chinese yam bulblet and millet porridge is an excellent **food therapy (食养)** for autumn and winter.

## Conclusion

> 「**The virtue of the Chinese yam is mildness and evenness; the merit of the Chinese yam is the three organs of Spleen, Lung, and Kidney.**」

The Chinese yam lacks the fierce impact of ginseng and antler, and the warming dryness of ginger and aconite;
it is simply an herb that is **unassuming, honest, and fit to be eaten long-term**.

If a few Iron-stick yams are kept on hand in the home,
morning porridge, midday soup, evening steamed cake,
day after day, **the Spleen and Stomach grow stronger, the Kidney qi is gradually replenished, and body and mind grow calm**.

This is the most beautiful form of "**medicine and food share the same origin (药食同源, yào shí tóng yuán)**" —
treating disease before it arises, nourishing life in three daily meals.
