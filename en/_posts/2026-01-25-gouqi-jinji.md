> 「Goji, sweet and neutral, enters the Liver and Kidney, benefits the Essence, brightens the eyes, and nourishes the Essence and blood.」
>
> ——Mnemonic verse from *Zhōngyào Xué* (《中药学》, Chinese Materia Medica)

Goji berries are the **ripe fruits of *Lycium barbarum***, a plant of the **Solanaceae (nightshade) family**.
The name first appears in the *Shījīng* (《诗经》, Book of Songs): "I climbed that northern hill, to gather the *qǐ* (杞, goji)."
Lù Yóu (陆游), the Southern Song poet, in his old age styled himself the "**Lay Practitioner of the Two Likes (二如居士, èr rú jū shì)**", and often drank goji porridge, living to the age of eighty-five.
He recorded in *Yùjí Zhāi Shūshì* (《玉笈斋书事》, Notes from the Jade-Case Studio): "After snow, the thatched hall is clear of bell and chime; at morning meal, a cup of goji broth."

A single small red fruit carries a thousand years of health-preservation history.
**But — have we really been eating it the right way?**

## I. The Nature, Flavor and Channel Entry of Goji Berries

- **Nature and flavor (性味)**: sweet, neutral
- **Channel entry (归经)**: enters the three meridians of **Liver, Kidney, and Lung (肝、肾、肺)**
- **Function (功效)**: **tonifies the Liver and Kidney, benefits the Essence and brightens the eyes, moistens the Lung (滋补肝肾、益精明目、润肺)**

> 「**Goji tonifies the Kidney and benefits the Essence; when the Water is flourishing, the bones are strong, and wasting-thirst, dim sight, lumbar pain, and knee pain are all healed.**」
>
> ——《本草通玄》(*Běncǎo Tōngxuán*, Penetrating the Mysteries of Materia Medica)

Please note several keywords:

- **Tonify (滋补)** — it is a "**nourishing (滋)** Yin" product, not a "**warming (温)** Yang" one
- **Moisten (润)** the Lung — it moistens without drying, of the same class as *Shāshēn* (沙参, Adenophora / Glehnia) and *Mài dōng* (麦冬, Ophiopogon)
- **Brighten the eyes (明目)** — the Liver opens into the eyes, the Kidney stores the Essence and nourishes the eyes; goji tonifies both the Liver and Kidney and so brightens the eyes

## II. Who Is Suitable to Eat Them

### 1. People with Liver–Kidney Yin Deficiency (肝肾阴虚)

- Sore lower back and knees, dizziness and tinnitus
- Blurry vision, dry and tearing eyes
- Premature greying of hair, insomnia and profuse dreams
- Tidal heat and night sweats, vexing heat in the five centers

### 2. People with Blood Depletion from Prolonged Eye Use (久视伤血)

- Those who stare at computers and phones for long hours
- Students, designers, programmers, writers

### 3. Middle-Aged and Elderly People of Balanced Constitution

- Premature aging, declining memory
- Lack of vigor, frequent night urination

### 4. People with Essence-Depletion Infertility (精亏不孕)

- Andrology: low sperm count, weak sperm, non-liquefied semen
- Gynecology: unstable *chōng rèn* (冲任, Thoroughfare and Conception vessels), habitual miscarriage (to be combined with other herbs)

## III. Who Should Not Eat Them — The Three "Don'ts"

### ❌ 1. Do Not Give Them to People with External-Contraction Excess Heat (外感实热)

During colds, fever, sore swollen throat, mouth ulcers, or acne flare-ups, **goji is contraindicated**.
At this time there is excess fire and pathogenic qi within the body; the sweet, moist nature of goji will **"close the door to keep the bandit inside" (闭门留寇)**, making the pathogenic factor hard to expel.

> In one sentence: **when pathogenic qi is in excess, first expel the pathogen, then tonify**.

### ❌ 2. Do Not Give Too Many to People with Spleen-Deficiency Dampness Excess (脾虚湿盛)

- White or yellow greasy tongue coating
- Stools sticky and ungratifying
- Heavy body, oppression in the chest and epigastrium
- Profuse phlegm, easy edema

These people already have dampness; the sweet, moist, cloying nature of goji will **aggravate the dampness**, producing "**excess fire (上火)**" or abdominal distension.

### ❌ 3. Do Not Give Pregnant Women Large Amounts

Pregnant women's diet should be light, balanced, and nutritious, but should not be heavily tonified.
Goji is neutral in nature; small amounts cause no harm; but if taken in large amounts, or combined with warming-hot medicines, it may disturb the *fetal qi* (胎气).
**Pregnant women should consult a physician before eating goji**.

## IV. Common Pairings of Goji Berries — The Three "Dos"

### ✅ 1. Do Combine with Yin-Nourishing Herbs to Boost the Effect

| Pairing | Effect | Indicated for |
|------|------|------|
| Goji + **Chrysanthemum (菊花)** | Nourishes Yin and brightens the eyes, clears the Liver and descends fire | Red, swollen, painful eyes, dizziness |
| Goji + **Cooked Rehmannia (熟地黄)** | Tonifies the Liver and Kidney, nourishes blood and fills the Essence | Sore lower back and knees, premature greying |
| Goji + **Chinese yam (山药)** | Tonifies the Kidney and strengthens the Spleen, boosts qi and nourishes Yin | Spleen–Kidney dual deficiency, persistent diarrhea |
| Goji + ***Shāshēn* (沙参)** | Moistens the Lung and stops cough, nourishes Yin and generates fluids | Dry cough with little phlegm, dry mouth |
| Goji + **Chrysanthemum + Cassia seed (菊花 + 决明子)** | Clears the Liver and brightens the eyes, lowers blood pressure and promotes bowel movement | Eye fatigue, hypertension |

### ✅ 2. Do Control the Dosage

> 「**Medicine used is like troops deployed; food used is like syrup dispensed; both have a measure — exceeding it brings disaster.**」

- **Daily health preservation**: **10–15 berries** (about 5–10 g) per day
- **Therapeutic conditioning**: **15–30 berries** (about 10–15 g) per day
- **Excessive dosage**: over 30 g per day may cause excess fire, abdominal distension, nosebleed

### ✅ 3. Do Use the Right Method

- **Chew (嚼食)**: simplest, most absorbing (chew slowly in small amounts)
- **Steep in water (泡水)**: water temperature **60–70 °C**; boiling water will destroy vitamins and pigments
- **Cook in porridge (煮粥)**: cook together with Chinese yam, polished rice, jujubes
- **Stew in soup (炖汤)**: stew with pork ribs, silkie chicken, white fungus
- **Soak in wine (泡酒)**: steep in **low-alcohol rice wine** (not for those with Yin-deficiency fire effulgence)

## V. Selection and Storage

### 1. By Place of Origin

- **Zhongning in Ningxia (宁夏中宁)** — the authentic Dao-di (道地) production area, plump berries, dark-red color, moderate sweetness
- **Qinghai (青海)** — large berries, bright color, but slightly less effective
- **Xinjiang (新疆)** — large berries, high sweetness, but inclined to warming

**The authentic (道地) is best**: Ningxia Zhongning goji is a National Geographical Indication Product of China.

### 2. By Appearance

- **Top grade**: uniform berries, dark-red color (not flashy), thick flesh, conspicuous white spot at the stem
- **Medium grade**: duller color, slightly smaller berries
- **Inferior grade**: overly bright red (possibly sulphur-fumigated), clumped together (possibly damp and spoiled)

### 3. Three Steps to Test Authenticity

- **Steep in water**: top-grade goji yields water of a **pale yellow**; dyed goji yields water that is **red**
- **Pinch with the fingers**: top-grade goji returns to its original shape after release; dyed goji feels sticky
- **Taste**: top-grade goji is sweet with a slight sourness, with a faintly bitter aftertaste; dyed goji is **purely sweet without aftertaste**

### 4. Storage Method

- **Sealed + refrigerated**: 0–4 °C, can be kept for half a year
- **Sealed + frozen**: –18 °C, can be kept for 1 year
- **Avoid**: damp, sunlight, contact with air

**Note**: damp goji easily breeds insects and molds.
In summer, after opening, it is recommended to **finish within two weeks**.

## VI. Several Common Food-Therapy Recipes

### 1. Goji Chrysanthemum Tea (枸杞菊花茶)

> 10 goji berries + 5 chrysanthemum flowers, brew in 60 °C warm water.

Suitable for: those who use their eyes for long periods, those with red, swollen, painful eyes, those with Liver fire.

### 2. Goji and Chinese Yam Porridge (枸杞山药粥)

> 15 goji berries + 50 g Chinese yam + 80 g polished rice, cook together into porridge.

Suitable for: those with Spleen–Stomach weakness and Liver–Kidney insufficiency; can be a regular breakfast.

### 3. Goji and Mulberry Paste (枸杞桑葚膏)

> 500 g goji + 500 g mulberry, decoct and concentrate, add honey to form a paste.

Suitable for: daily nourishment of middle-aged and elderly (diabetics should omit the honey).

### 4. Goji Stewed with Black-Bone Chicken (枸杞炖乌鸡)

> 20 g goji + half a silkie chicken + 5 jujubes + 3 slices of ginger, stew for 2 hours.

Suitable for: post-partum, post-illness, post-surgery conditioning.

## Conclusion

> 「**Superior-grade medicines nurture the destiny; those in correspondence with Heaven may be taken long-term.**」

Goji is a superior-grade herb, but superior-grade is **not a magic medicine**.
It merely **adds a touch of warm, moistening tonification to the plain, day-after-day diet**.

Eating goji correctly is not "the more the better",
but **clearly discerning one's constitution, choosing the right pairing, and controlling the dosage**.

May you, each day, take a small handful — **brightening the eyes, nourishing the Liver, enriching the Kidney, quieting the spirit**,
**slowly, is faster (慢慢来，比较快)**.
