> 「好雨知时节，当春乃发生。随风潜入夜，润物细无声。」
>
> "The good rain knows the seasons; when spring comes, it gives life. It slips in with the wind at night, and moistens all things without a sound."
>
> ——Du Fu (杜甫, Dù Fǔ), «Spring Night's Happy Rain» (《春夜喜雨》)

Yǔshuǐ (雨水, Yǔshuǐ, Rain Water) is the **second solar term (节气, jiéqì) after Lichun (立春)**.
The *Yuèlìng Qīshí'èr Hòu Jíjiě* (《月令七十二候集解》, Collection of Explanations of the Seventy-two Pentads of the Monthly Ordinances) says: "In the first month of the year, Heaven gives birth to water; spring begins to belong to wood, yet what gives birth to wood must be water; therefore Lichun is followed by Yǔshuǐ."
The east wind thaws the ice, then scatters it as rain. **Heaven and Earth are in mutual openness, and dampness (湿, shī) waxes strong.**

## I. The Three Pentads of Yǔshuǐ: Knowing Its Waxing and Waning

- **First pentad — The otter sacrifices fish (獭祭鱼):** the otter catches fish and lays them out by the water's edge, as if in sacrifice — animals respond to the season and know how to advance.
- **Second pentad — Wild geese come (鸿雁来):** the great geese fly from south to north, migrating with the rising yang.
- **Third pentad — The grass and trees begin to sprout (草木萌动):** Heaven and Earth are in mutual openness; the grass and trees, following the rising yang qi (阳气, yángqì) of the earth, begin to sprout.

The images of all three pentads remind us: **the yang of Heaven is already stirring, and the dampness of Earth is already rising**.
In nourishing life, one should **raise the clear yang (清阳, qīngyáng) and transform the turbid dampness (湿浊, shīzhuó)**.

## II. Six Signs of Spleen Encumbered by Dampness

After Yǔshuǐ, if the following symptoms appear, it is most often **spleen deficiency with damp encumbrance (脾虚湿困, píxū shīkùn)**:

1. **Head heavy as if wrapped (头重如裹):** the head feels dull and heavy, as if wrapped in a damp towel.
2. **Limb fatigue (四肢乏力):** one is always weary, and does not feel refreshed even after rising.
3. **Poor appetite (胃口不开):** food has no fragrance; after eating, the belly bloats.
4. **Stool sticky and greasy (大便黏腻):** stool sticks to the toilet and is hard to flush clean.
5. **Tongue coating white and greasy (舌苔白腻):** a thick white "frost" covers the tongue.
6. **Skin sticky and greasy (皮肤黏腻):** the body always feels sticky and unrefreshing.

**"All dampness, swelling and fullness belongs to the Spleen (诸湿肿满，皆属于脾)"** —
though damp disease involves the Lungs and Kidneys as well, the **Spleen (脾)** is the key.
The Spleen governs the transportation and transformation of water-dampness; when the Spleen is deficient, water-dampness pools, and where dampness pools, a hundred diseases arise.

## III. The Four Treasures of Food Therapy to Dispel Dampness

### 1. Yìmǐ (薏米, Coix Seed / Job's Tears)

Coix seed is cool in nature, sweet and bland in flavor, enters the meridians of Spleen, Stomach and Lung; it **leaches out dampness (利水渗湿) and fortifies the Spleen to stop diarrhea (健脾止泻)**.
- Stir-fried yìmǐ (stir-fried until slightly yellow) is slightly warm, suited to those with spleen-stomach deficiency-cold.
- Raw yìmǐ is slightly cool, suited to damp-heat (湿热, shīrè) constitutions.

**Recommended food therapy:** Coix Seed and Chìxiǎodòu Congee (薏米赤小豆粥)
> Stir-fried yìmǐ 30 g + chìxiǎodòu (赤小豆, Phaseolus calcaratus, "red rice bean") 30 g + qiànshí (芡实, Euryale ferox seed) 10 g; soak for 2 hours, then cook together.
> Note: chìxiǎodòu is *not* the common red kidney bean (hóngxiǎodòu).

### 2. Shānyào (山药, Chinese Yam / Dioscorea opposita)

> "Shānyào benefits people; it tonifies the Spleen and Lung, and secures kidney essence."

Shānyào is neutral in nature, sweet in flavor; it **tonifies the Spleen and nourishes the Stomach, generates fluids and benefits the Lung, tonifies the Kidneys and astringes the essence (补脾养胃、生津益肺、补肾涩精)**.
It can be steamed, boiled, stir-fried, or made into soup; it is **the foremost choice for gently tonifying the Spleen and Stomach (平补脾胃)**.

**Recommended:** Iron-stick yam (铁棍山药) and millet congee, one bowl each morning — better than ginseng or velvet antler.

### 3. Fúlíng (茯苓, Poria cocos)

Fúlíng is the **"Divine Medicine of the Four Seasons" (四时神药)** — neither cold nor hot, neither moistening nor drying.
Neutral in nature, sweet and bland in flavor, it enters the meridians of Heart, Lung, Spleen and Kidney.
It fortifies the Spleen and leaches out dampness, calms the Heart and quiets the spirit; it is the archetype of **"dispelling dampness without damaging the upright qi" (「去湿不伤正」)**.

**Recommended:** fúlíng cakes (茯苓饼), fúlíng cakes (茯苓糕), or fúlíng powder stirred into water as a daily tea.

### 4. Qiànshí (芡实, Euryale ferox Seed / Gordon Euryale Seed)

> "When infants eat it, they do not age; when the elderly eat it, they prolong their years."

Qiànshí tonifies the Spleen and dispels dampness, benefits the Kidneys and secures the essence, similar to liánzǐ (莲子, lotus seed); it is **neutral in nature and not drying (性平不燥)**.
**Recommended:** Sìshéntāng (四神汤, "Four Spirits Soup") — shānyào, liánzǐ, qiànshí, and fúlíng, 10 g each; it can be stewed with meat or cooked into congee.

## IV. Moxibustion on Three Points to Raise Yang and Transform Dampness

At the Yǔshuǐ term, although the yang of Heaven and Earth is rising, the damp-cold at ground level has not yet retreated.
**Moxibustion (艾灸, àijiǔ)** warms yang and transforms dampness, most fitting for the season.

### 1. Zhōngwǎn (中脘, CV-12)

- **Location:** 4 *cùn* (寸) directly above the navel (about 5 finger-widths)
- **Effects:** harmonize the Stomach and fortify the Spleen, transform dampness and descend counterflow
- **Moxibustion method:** mild-warm moxibustion 15-20 minutes, 2-3 times per week

### 2. Zúsānlǐ (足三里, ST-36)

- **Location:** 3 *cùn* below the outer eye of the knee, one finger-width lateral to the tibia
- **Effects:** fortify the Spleen and harmonize the Stomach, support the upright and cultivate the original
- **Moxibustion method:** mild-warm moxibustion 10-15 minutes, until the skin flushes

### 3. Yīnlíngquán (阴陵泉, SP-9)

- **Location:** on the inner side of the lower leg, in the depression below the medial condyle of the tibia
- **Effects:** **the foremost point for fortifying the Spleen and dispelling dampness (健脾祛湿第一穴)**
- **Moxibustion method:** mild-warm moxibustion 10-15 minutes

**Best time:** 9-11 a.m. is optimal (the time when the Spleen meridian is most active / 脾经当令).
**After moxibustion:** avoid wind and cold; do not bathe or drink cold beverages for 4 hours.

## V. Daoyin (导引, dǎoyǐn): "Spleen-Stomach Breathing Method"

A simple little practice, 5 minutes upon rising in the morning:

1. **Stand or sit**, relax the whole body
2. **Breathe in slowly**, imagining the clear yang (清阳) qi entering through the nose, rising along the spine to the top of the head
3. **Breathe out slowly**, imagining the turbid damp (浊湿) qi leaving through the mouth, descending along the chest, entering the earth through the Yǒngquán (涌泉, KD-1) points of both feet
4. Each inhale-exhale is about 6-8 seconds; do 20-30 breaths

This method derives from **Mǎwángduī Daoyin (马王堆导引术, Mǎwángduī Daoyin Exercises)**, and can raise the clear and descend the turbid, fortify the Spleen and transform dampness.
Persist for one month, and the tongue coating will thin and head and eyes feel refreshed.

## VI. Daily Routine: Avoid Damp, Keep Away from Cold

After Yǔshuǐ, as air humidity increases, pay attention to:

- ❌ Change "**go to bed early, rise early**" to "**sleep at night, rise early**" (夜卧早起), following the yang qi
- ❌ Keep the dwelling **free of dampness**; place some bamboo charcoal or moisture-absorbing packets in the corners
- ❌ **Do not get caught in the rain** on rainy days; change clothes promptly if you do
- ❌ After **exercise**, wait for the sweat to dry before bathing, otherwise dampness enters the *còu lǐ* (腠理, interstices)
- ✅ Frequently **sun the clothes and bedding**; the sun is the fire of Heaven, and is most able to transform dampness
- ✅ **Keep the rooms well ventilated**, but close up on smoggy or rainy days

## Conclusion

Yǔshuǐ: **rain descends from Heaven, dampness rises from Earth**.
The rain of Heaven, we must follow its course and nourish accordingly;
the damp of Earth, we must fortify the Spleen to transform.

> 「**脾为后天之本，气血生化之源。**» ("The Spleen is the root of postnatal life, the source of qi and blood transformation.")

Nourish the Spleen and Stomach well, and you nourish the whole body's "mechanism of transportation and transformation (运化之机)".
This pair of characters "yùn huà (运化, transportation and transformation)" is precisely the greatest teaching that the Yǔshuǐ solar term offers us.
