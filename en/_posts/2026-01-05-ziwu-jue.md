> 「**At the first quarter of the *zǐ* hour, the first Yang is born.**」
>
> 「**At the first quarter of the *wǔ* hour, the first Yin is born.**」
>
> ——《黄帝内经》

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) speaks of **"Heaven–Human correspondence" (天人相应)** —
Heaven has twelve *shíchén* (时辰, two-hour periods), and humans have twelve meridians cycling through them; Heaven has the waxing and waning of Yin and Yang, and humans have the rise and fall of qi (气, qì) and blood.

Among the twelve two-hour periods, the ***zǐ* hour (子时, 23:00–01:00)** and the ***wǔ* hour (午时, 11:00–13:00)** are the two key moments of **Yin–Yang junction (阴阳交接)**:
- At the *zǐ* hour, **Yin reaches its utmost and Yang is born** — the first Yang returns
- At the *wǔ* hour, **Yang reaches its utmost and Yin is born** — the first Yin emerges

To sleep through these two periods is to comply with the timing of Heaven — the cheapest, most effective way to **harmonize Yin and Yang (调和阴阳)**.

## I. What Is the Ziwu Sleep

The **Ziwu Sleep (子午觉)** is sleep at the two periods of the ***zǐ* hour (子时)** and the ***wǔ* hour (午时)**:

- **The *zǐ* hour sleep**: sleep at night, 23:00–01:00
- **The *wǔ* hour nap**: a brief rest at midday, 11:00–13:00

The ancients said: **"A good Ziwu sleep beats tonic medicine" (睡好子午觉，赛过吃补药)** — a wisdom distilled by working people from a thousand years of practice.

### 1. Why One Should Sleep at the *Zǐ* Hour

The *zǐ* hour (23:00–01:00) is when the **Gallbladder Meridian (胆经, Dǎnjīng) is on duty**.

> 「**The eleven viscera all depend on the Gallbladder.**」——《素问·六节藏象论》(*Sùwèn*, *Liùjiē Zàngxiàng Lùn*)

The *zǐ* hour is the moment in the day when **Yin qi (阴气) is at its fullest and Yang qi (阳气) is just being born**.
At this time, the qi and blood of the **Gallbladder Meridian (胆经, Dǎnjīng) are at their peak**, and **Yang qi begins to sprout**;
if one is awake then, **the sprouting of Yang qi is obstructed**, and the next day brings listlessness, dizziness, and a foggy head.

> 「**Not sleeping at the *zǐ* hour, blood cannot return to the Gallbladder.**」

The cost of not sleeping at the *zǐ* hour:

- ❌ **Insufficiency of Gallbladder qi (胆气不足)** — diminished decisiveness, indecisiveness
- ❌ **Abnormal secretion of bile (胆汁分泌异常)** — affecting digestion, bitter taste in the mouth
- ❌ **Insufficient rise of Yang qi (阳气生发不足)** — fatigue and drowsiness the next day
- ❌ **Long-term failure to sleep at the *zǐ* hour** — prone to liver and gallbladder disease, neurasthenia

### 2. Why One Should Sleep at the *Wǔ* Hour

The *wǔ* hour (11:00–13:00) is when the **Heart Meridian (心经, Xīnjīng) is on duty**.

> 「**The heart is the organ of the sovereign; the bright spirit issues from it.**」

The *wǔ* hour is the moment in the day when **Yang qi is at its fullest and Yin qi is just being born**.
A short nap at this time, complying with the principle of "Yin engendered at the utmost of Yang", can:

- ✅ **Nourish heart Yin (养心阴)** — when heart Yin is nourished, the sovereign is not disturbed
- ✅ **Descend heart fire (降心火)** — Yin being engendered can restrain the over-exuberant Yang
- ✅ **Nourish heart blood (养心血)** — when heart blood is filled, the spirit is clear
- ✅ **Restore energy (恢复精力)** — the afternoon is full of vigor

> 「**A short rest at the *wǔ* hour — better than being an immortal.**」

The cost of not sleeping at the *wǔ* hour:

- ❌ **Hyperactive heart fire (心火亢盛)** — vexation, insomnia, profuse dreaming
- ❌ **Drowsiness in the afternoon** — efficiency drops
- ❌ **Long-term accumulation** — increased risk of cardiovascular and cerebrovascular disease

## II. The Three Major Health Benefits of the Ziwu Sleep

### 1. Harmonizing Yin and Yang (调和阴阳)

> 「**When Yin is calm and Yang is stored, the spirit is well regulated.**」

At the *zǐ* hour, the first Yang is born; at the *wǔ* hour, the first Yin is born.
**Sleeping at the *zǐ* hour** aids the rise of Yang qi; **sleeping at the *wǔ* hour** aids the growth of Yin qi.
**When Yin and Yang are in harmony, the hundred diseases do not arise**.

### 2. Nurturing the Heart and the Gallbladder

- **Nurturing the Gallbladder at the *zǐ* hour**: when Gallbladder qi is ample, decisiveness is strong; when Gallbladder qi is weak, one is indecisive
- **Nurturing the Heart at the *wǔ* hour**: when Heart qi is ample, the spirit is clear; when Heart qi is weak, there are palpitations and insomnia

### 3. Restoring Vigor

- **Deep sleep at the *zǐ* hour** — full of energy the next day
- **A brief nap at the *wǔ* hour** — clear mind in the afternoon, efficiency increased

## III. The "Right Way" of the Ziwu Sleep

### 🌙 Sleep at the *Zǐ* Hour

#### When to Sleep

- **Best time to fall asleep**: **22:30–23:00**
- **Best time to wake up**: **05:00–07:00** (after sunrise)
- **Total sleep duration**: **7–8 hours**

#### How to Prepare

- **One hour before bed**: avoid phones, computers, and TV (blue light injures Yin)
- **Half an hour before bed**: soak feet in warm water, listen to light music, meditate
- **At bedtime**: perform small exercises such as **tapping the teeth (叩齿), rubbing Yǒngquán (KD-1, 涌泉), and kneading the abdomen (揉腹)**
- **Avoid**: strong tea, coffee, vigorous exercise, eating too much, going hungry

#### Sleeping Posture

- **Recommended**: **lying on the right side** — "**Sleeping like a bow (卧如弓)**"
- **Reason**: the heart is on the left; lying on the right does not compress the heart
- **Pillow**: appropriate height, **with support under the neck**
- **Bedding**: **should not be too thick**; **the feet may be kept slightly warmer**

#### Sleep Environment

- ✅ **Avoid light**: draw the curtains, **out of sight of light**
- ✅ **Quiet**: silent or white noise
- ✅ **Room temperature**: **18–22 °C** is ideal
- ✅ **Air circulation**: open a window or use a fresh-air system
- ❌ **Do not** sleep with the head covered — oxygen deprivation injures qi
- ❌ **Do not** sleep with the mouth open — depletes fluids and qi

### ☀️ Sleep at the *Wǔ* Hour

#### When to Sleep

- **Best nap time**: **11:00–13:00**
- **Best duration**: **20–30 minutes** (**not exceeding 1 hour**)

#### Why 20–30 Minutes

- About 20 minutes: **light sleep** (stages N1–N2)
- About 30 minutes: **deep sleep** (stage N3)
- Over 1 hour: enter **rapid eye movement (REM)**; **waking leaves you drowsier**
- Over 1.5 hours: complete a **full sleep cycle**

> 「**A quarter-hour of midday sleep beats a whole night of work.**」

#### How to Nap

- **Posture**: **lying down is best**; if unable to lie down, **reclining in a chair** is better than lying face-down
- **Note**: **do not lie face-down** — it compresses the eyeballs, impairs digestion, and strains the neck
- **Environment**: quiet, dark, comfortable room temperature
- **Do not** sleep immediately after a meal — wait **15–30 minutes after eating** before napping
- **Do not** nap too late — **no more naps after 3:00 PM**, or nighttime sleep will be affected

#### After Waking

- ✅ **Rise slowly**, **sit for 1 minute** before standing
- ✅ **Drink a little warm water**
- ✅ **Step outside** and walk for 2–3 minutes
- ❌ **Do not** plunge into work immediately
- ❌ **Do not** drive right away (reaction time is slow just after waking)

## IV. The Ziwu Sleep for Special Groups

### 1. Students

- **Ziwu sleep**: must get enough sleep, **be in bed before 22:00**
- **Wǔ hour nap**: **20–30 minutes** is ideal
- **Effect**: **memory improves**, **afternoon study efficiency** clearly enhanced

### 2. Office Workers

- **Ziwu sleep**: try to be asleep by 23:00
- **Wǔ hour nap**: a **20-minute rest** in the office
- **Note**: **do not** use coffee to replace the nap; **caffeine** affects nighttime sleep

### 3. The Elderly

- **Ziwu sleep**: **early to bed, early to rise**, comply with Yang qi
- **Wǔ hour nap**: **30–60 minutes** (the elderly may extend it a little)
- **Effect**: **lowers the risk of cardiovascular and cerebrovascular disease**

### 4. Infants and Young Children

- **Ziwu sleep**: best to be asleep **before 21:00**
- **Wǔ hour nap**: **1–3 hours** (according to age)
- **Effect**: **promotes secretion of growth hormone** (growth hormone peaks in deep sleep at night)

### 5. Insomniacs

- **Ziwu sleep**: especially important; **must be asleep before 23:00**
- **Wǔ hour nap**: **do not nap, or no more than 20 minutes**
- **Reason**: **reduces the sleep drive**, making it easier to fall asleep at night

## V. Common Misconceptions About the Ziwu Sleep

### ❌ Misconception 1: "Catch-up Sleep" on Weekends

Staying up late during the week, then sleeping in on the weekend, **wrecks the biological clock**.
Correct approach: **maintain a regular *zǐ* hour sleep** (fall asleep 22:30–23:00).

### ❌ Misconception 2: The Longer the Nap, the Better

Napping for more than 1 hour leads to **waking up even sleepier** and causes **nighttime insomnia**.
Correct approach: **20–30 minutes** is best.

### ❌ Misconception 3: Sleeping Right After a Meal

After eating, blood is concentrated in the stomach for digestion, and **the brain is short of blood**,
so sleeping immediately **easily leads to indigestion and stroke**.
Correct approach: wait **15–30 minutes after eating** before napping.

### ❌ Misconception 4: Dozing in a Chair as a Substitute for a Nap

Dozing in a chair leaves you **more tired on waking**, **stiff in the neck**, and **short of blood to the brain**.
Correct approach: **try to lie down** for the nap, or **recline in a chair**.

### ❌ Misconception 5: Being "Too Particular" About the Nap Keeps You Awake

- **Too bright** — wear an eye mask
- **Too noisy** — use earplugs / white noise
- **Bed too soft** — switch to a slightly firmer nap pillow
- **Time too short** — plan **30 minutes** of rest in advance

## VI. Small Exercises to Pair with the Ziwu Sleep

### 1. Before the *Zǐ* Hour: Foot Soak + Rub Yǒngquán (KD-1, 涌泉)

- 30 minutes before bed, soak feet in **40–45 °C hot water** for 20 minutes
- Then rub **Yǒngquán (KD-1, 涌泉穴)** 100 times
- **"Drawing fire back to the origin (引火归元)"** — the strongest aid to sleep

### 2. Before the *Zǐ* Hour: Tap the Teeth + Swallow the Saliva

- Tap the teeth 36 times
- Stir the tongue 36 times
- Swish in the mouth 36 times
- **Swallow in 3 portions**, sending the saliva down to the Dāntián (丹田)

### 3. After Waking at the *Wǔ* Hour: Press and Knead Nèiguān (PC-6) + Shénmén (HT-7)

- **Nèiguān (内关穴, PC-6)** (2 *cùn* above the wrist crease): press and knead for 1 minute
- **Shénmén (神门穴, HT-7)** (ulnar end of the wrist crease): press and knead for 1 minute
- Refreshing and awakening, **nurturing the heart and quieting the spirit (养心安神)**

### 4. After Waking from the Nap: Take a Walk Outdoors

- **Outdoor sunlight** resets the biological clock
- **Walking 2–3 minutes** promotes blood circulation
- Avoid the harm of "**waking up and going straight to work**"

## VII. When to See a Doctor

- ⚠️ **Long-term difficulty falling asleep** (over 30 minutes) lasting more than 1 month
- ⚠️ **Waking easily at night** and unable to fall back asleep
- ⚠️ **Early waking** (waking at 2:00–4:00 AM) and feeling tired afterward
- ⚠️ **Severe daytime drowsiness** affecting work and life
- ⚠️ **Severe snoring** with **apnea** (possibly sleep apnea syndrome)
