> 「春三月，此谓发陈，天地俱生，万物以荣。」
>
> "The three months of spring are the season of *fā chén* (发陈, the outpouring of what is stored). Heaven and Earth together give birth; the ten thousand things flourish."
>
> ——《Huángdì Nèijīng》(《黄帝内经》, Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon), *Sùwèn* (《素问》, Basic Questions), *Sìqì Tiáoshén Dàlùn* (《四气调神大论》, Great Treatise on Regulating the Spirit with the Four Qi)

The three months of spring are **the six solar terms of Lichun (立春, Beginning of Spring), Yǔshuǐ (雨水, Rain Water), Jīngzhé (惊蛰, Awakening of Insects), Chūnfēn (春分, Spring Equinox), Qīngmíng (清明, Clear and Bright) and Gǔyǔ (谷雨, Grain Rain)**.
Heaven and Earth give birth together, the ten thousand things pour forth; the Liver of the human body also responds and **generates, courses, and discharges (生发、疏泄)**.

> 「**肝者，将军之官，谋虑出焉。**」 ("The Liver is the office of the general; from it spring planning and strategy.")

The Liver is the general, **its nature loves free coursing (条达) and hates depression (抑郁)**.
The qi of spring enters the Liver; the root of yangsheng (养生) is in nourishing the Liver, and the root of nourishing the Liver is in **"shū xiè" (疏泄, free coursing and discharge)**.

## I. The Three Major Functions of the Liver

To understand the Liver, you must be able to nourish it. The Liver has three major functions:

### 1. Governs Free Coursing (主疏泄)

**Shū (疏)** — to make the qi movement smooth; **xiè (泄)** — to make constraint and depression disperse.
When the Liver's free-coursing function is normal, then:

- ✅ Emotions are free, neither depressed nor angry
- ✅ The Spleen and Stomach transport and transform, digestion is normal
- ✅ Qi and Blood circulate, the sinews are supple
- ✅ Menstruation comes on time, with normal amount and color

When free coursing fails, **a hundred diseases are born**.

### 2. Stores Blood (主藏血)

The Liver is the **"sea of blood" (血海)**, capable of storing blood and regulating its volume.
> 「**人卧则血归于肝。**」 ("When a person lies down, the blood returns to the Liver.") — *Sùwèn* (《素问》, Basic Questions), *Wǔzàng Shēngchéng* (《五脏生成》, Treatise on the Generation of the Five Zang)

During night sleep, blood flows back to the Liver, **and Liver blood is nourished (肝血得以滋养)**;
during the day's activity, Liver blood is released to supply the whole body.
**Those who stay up late suffer from insufficient Liver blood, which most damages its root.**

### 3. Governs the Sinews; Its Manifestation Is in the Nails (主筋，其华在爪)

Liver blood nourishes the sinews, **and supple sinews can bind the bones and benefit the joints (筋柔则能束骨而利关节)**.
The nails are the "surplus of the sinews"; with sufficient Liver blood, the nails are rosy, tough, and lustrous.
**Those with many vertical ridges, easily broken, or pale** are mostly those with insufficient Liver blood.

## II. Six Keys to Nourishing the Liver in Spring

### 🌱 Key One: Daily Routine — "Sleep Late, Rise Early, Loose Hair and Loose Body"

- **Sleep late:** go to bed at the *hài* hour (亥时, 21:00-23:00), to nourish Liver blood
- **Rise early:** rise at the *mǎo* hour (卯时, 5:00-7:00), to follow the Liver's rising
- **Loose hair and loose body (披发缓形):** do not bind the body, let the qi movement unfold
- **Stride through the courtyard (广步于庭):** take a walk on rising, **eyes following a flying swan (目送飞鸿)**, let Liver qi unfold

Strictly avoid:

- ❌ Lying in bed until noon — it obstructs the rise and generation of Liver qi
- ❌ Staying up till the small hours — it consumes Liver blood
- ❌ Sitting all day — qi and blood stagnate

### 🌿 Key Two: Diet — "Reduce Sour, Increase Sweet, Pungent and Dispersing"

> 「**肝苦急，急食甘以缓之。**」 ("The Liver hates urgency; urgently eat sweet to slow it.")

In the spring months Liver qi tends to be hyperactive; **too much sour makes Liver qi overly hyperactive, and counter-restrains spleen earth (过酸则肝气过亢，反克脾土)**.
Therefore in spring one should **"reduce sour, increase sweet" (少酸增甘)**, using more **pungent-sweet and dispersing (辛甘发散)** items, to help the Liver qi rise and generate.

**Recommended in spring:**

| Food | Effect | Recommended preparation |
|------|--------|-------------------------|
| Spinach (菠菜) | Nourishes blood and moistens dryness | Cold-tossed, blanched and stir-fried |
| Chinese chives (韭菜) | Warms the middle and assists yang | Chive pockets, scrambled eggs with chives |
| Chinese toon (香椿) | Courses the Liver and fortifies the Stomach | Toon scrambled with egg, toon with cold tofu |
| Spring bamboo shoots (春笋) | Rises and generates yang qi | Braised spring bamboo shoots |
| Jujube (大枣) | Tonifies the Spleen and nourishes blood | In congee, in soup |
| Chinese yam (山药) | Tonifies the Spleen and benefits the Kidneys | Chinese yam and millet congee |
| Shepherd's purse (荠菜) | Clears the Liver and brightens the eyes | Shepherd's purse dumplings, shepherd's purse and tofu soup |
| Scallion and garlic (葱蒜) | Frees yang and disperses cold | Spring pancakes, seasoning |

**Eat less in spring:**

- ❌ Excessively sour (vinegar, plums, hawthorn, etc.) — over-astringing, hinders the Liver's rise
- ❌ Greasy, heavy flavors — increases the Liver's coursing burden
- ❌ Cold and raw, chilled foods — damages Spleen yang, earth weakened and wood wavers
- ❌ Alcohol — directly injures the Liver

### 🌸 Key Three: Emotions — "Refrain from Anger, Refrain from Depression"

> 「**大怒伤肝。**」 ("Great anger injures the Liver.") — *Sùwèn* (《素问》, Basic Questions), *Yīnyáng Yìngxiàng Dàlùn* (《阴阳应象大论》, Great Treatise on the Correspondences of Yin and Yang)

The Liver governs free coursing, and **emotional dissatisfaction (情志不遂)** is the most common cause of Liver disease.
In the spring months, Liver qi is naturally vigorous; if one adds depression and anger, one easily gets:

- Dizziness and headache (liver yang hyperactivity, 肝阳上亢)
- Distension and pain in the chest and flanks (liver qi constraint, 肝气郁结)
- Menstrual irregularity (liver constraint with blood stasis, 肝郁血瘀)
- Insomnia and dream-disturbed sleep (liver constraint transforming into fire, 肝郁化火)

**Four points for Liver-nourishing emotions:**

1. **Go out into nature more** — mountains, waters, and forests best soothe the Liver
2. **Gather with friends more** — confiding and conversation allow Liver qi to relax
3. **Listen to *jué* notes (角音, mi)** — the five-element music that courses the Liver
4. **Reduce solitude and repression** — cry when you need to, laugh when you need to

### ☯ Key Four: The Meridians — "Tap the Gallbladder Meridian, Press Tàichōng (LV-3)"

#### 1. Tap the Gallbladder Meridian (敲胆经)

On the outer side of the thigh, at the trouser seam, tap 200 times daily.
**Tapping the Gallbladder Meridian promotes the Liver and Gallbladder's free-coursing function (促进肝胆的疏泄功能)**.

#### 2. Press the Tàichōng Point (揉太冲穴)

Tàichōng (太冲, LR-3) is **the source point (原穴) of the Liver Meridian**:
- Location: on the dorsum of the foot, in the depression two finger-widths above the web between the great toe and the second toe
- Technique: press and knead with the thumb, 3 minutes per side, **until a sore, distended sensation is reached (至酸胀为度)**
- Timing: 1-2 times daily, **especially after losing one's temper**

#### 3. Qīmén Point (期门穴)

- Location: on the chest, directly below the nipple, in the 6th intercostal space
- Technique: press and knead 2-3 minutes
- Effect: courses the Liver and regulates qi, harmonizes Liver and Spleen

#### 4. Xíngjiān Point (行间穴)

- Location: on the dorsum of the foot, between the great toe and the second toe, at the posterior border of the web margin, at the red-white flesh junction (赤白肉际)
- Technique: press and knead 1-2 minutes
- Effect: clears the Liver and drains fire

### 🏃 Key Five: Exercise — "Stretch Sinews and Bones"

In spring, "**stride through the courtyard, loose hair and loose body (广步于庭，被发缓形)**" is apt.
Exercises most suitable for spring:

- ✅ **Walking:** 30 minutes daily, with a light sweat
- ✅ **Jogging:** in early morning or evening, within your capacity
- ✅ **Tai Chi (太极拳):** balances hardness and softness, best nourishes Liver qi
- ✅ **Eight Pieces of Brocade (八段锦):** has particularly good effects for coursing the Liver and regulating qi
- ✅ **Kite-flying (放风筝):** looking up into the distance, **best nourishes the eyes and protects the Liver**
- ✅ **Stretching exercises:** stretching sinews, yoga, softens the Liver and relaxes the sinews

**Avoid:**

- ❌ Sweating profusely — "**sweat is the fluid of the Heart; excessive sweating injures yang (汗为心液，过汗伤阳)**"
- ❌ Strenuous exercise at night — affects sleep, injures Liver blood
- ❌ Breath-holding and straining — increases the Liver's burden

### 🍵 Key Six: Medicinal Teas and Food Therapy

#### 1. Rose Flower Tea (玫瑰花茶)

> Rose flowers 5 pieces + jasmine flowers 3 pieces, steeped in boiling water.

**Courses the Liver and resolves depression, harmonizes blood and stops pain (疏肝解郁、和血止痛)**.
Suited for: those with emotional depression, distension and pain in the chest and flanks, menstrual irregularity.

#### 2. Chrysanthemum and Cassia Seed Tea (菊花决明子茶)

> Chrysanthemum 5 flowers + jué míng zǐ (决明子, Cassia seed) 10 g + gǒuqǐ (枸杞, Lycium) 10 berries, steeped in boiling water.

**Clears the Liver and brightens the eyes, moistens the intestines and unblocks the stool (清肝明目、润肠通便)**.
Suited for: those with red and swollen eyes, constipation, hypertension.

#### 3. Fóshǒu and Chénpí Drink (佛手陈皮饮)

> Fóshǒu (佛手, Buddha's hand / Citrus medica var. sarcodactylis) 10 g + chénpí (陈皮, aged tangerine peel) 6 g + rose flowers 5 pieces, decocted in water as a tea.

**Courses the Liver and harmonizes the Stomach, regulates qi and transforms phlegm (疏肝和胃、理气化痰)**.
Suited for: Liver-Stomach disharmony, chest oppression and belching, profuse phlegm.

#### 4. Cháihú Shūgān Sǎn (柴胡疏肝散, Bupleurum Liver-Coursing Powder, patent medicine)

> 「**Cháihú Shūgān Sǎn (柴胡疏肝散)**」: cháihú (柴胡, Bupleurum), báisháo (白芍, white peony), zhǐké (枳壳, bitter orange), xiāngfù (香附, Cyperus), chuānxiōng (川芎, Szechuan lovage), chénpí (陈皮, aged tangerine peel), gāncǎo (甘草, licorice).

**Courses the Liver and regulates qi, activates blood and stops pain (疏肝理气、活血止痛)**.
Suited for: those with obvious Liver qi constraint (**requires a physician's prescription**).

## IV. When to See a Doctor

If the following conditions appear, prompt medical attention is required:

- ⚠️ Jaundice (yellow eyes, yellow skin, yellow urine)
- ⚠️ Persistent right upper abdominal pain
- ⚠️ Extreme fatigue, nausea, loss of appetite
- ⚠️ Itchy skin, spider nevi
- ⚠️ Ascites, lower limb edema

In TCM, Liver disease is treated with methods of **"coursing the Liver, nourishing the Liver, clearing the Liver, draining the Liver" (疏肝、养肝、清肝、泻肝)**;
in Western medicine, Liver disease is treated with **"protecting the Liver, fighting viruses, fighting fibrosis" (保肝、抗病毒、抗纤维化)**.
**Integration of Chinese and Western medicine (中西医结合) is presently the most dependable choice.**

## Conclusion

Nourishing the Liver in spring is the opening move of the year's yangsheng.
To nourish the Liver well is to nourish **the source of the body's qi movement (一身气机的源头)**.

May you this spring,
**bright-eyed, clear-hearted, harmonious in feeling, smooth in qi, with a healthy Liver (眼明、心亮、情舒、气畅、肝健)**.

A year of good state begins in spring.
