> 「**大怒伤肝。**」
>
> 「**怒则气上。**」
>
> "Great anger injures the Liver."
>
> "In anger, qi rises."
>
> — *Huángdì Nèijīng* (《黄帝内经》, Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon), *Sùwèn* (《素问》, Basic Questions), *Yīnyáng Yìngxiàng Dàlùn* (《阴阳应象大论》, Great Treatise on the Correspondences of Yin and Yang)

In Chinese medicine, **anger (怒, nù)** is the **emotion of the Liver (肝志, gān zhì)**.
In anger, **qi rises (气上, qì shàng)**; when qi rises, **blood rushes upward following qi (血随气涌, xuè suí qì yǒng)**.
In mild cases: flushed face and red ears, dizziness and headache; in severe cases: **syncope, stroke, hematemesis**.

> 「**百病皆生于气。**」 ("A hundred diseases all arise from qi.")

And **anger (怒) is the chief of the hundred qi**.
The first task of nourishing life lies **not in what one eats, drinks, or supplements with**,
but in **«restraining anger» (节怒, jié nù)**.

## I. The Three Major Harms of Anger

### 1. Injures the Liver

> 「**肝在志为怒。**」 ("The Liver, in terms of emotion, is anger.")

- **In anger, qi rises** — Liver qi (肝气, gān qì) ascends excessively, becoming **liver yang hyperactivity (肝阳上亢, gān yáng shàng kàng)**
- **Mild symptoms:** flushed face and red eyes, dizziness and headache, bitter taste in the mouth, dry throat
- **Severe symptoms:** **internal stirring of liver wind (肝风内动, gān fēng nèi dòng)**, **stroke and syncope**
- **Chronic conditions:** **liver constraint transforming into fire (肝郁化火, gān yù huà huǒ)**, **horizontal counterflow of liver qi (肝气横逆, gān qì héng nì)**, **Liver-Spleen disharmony (肝脾不调, gān pí bù tiáo)**

### 2. Injures the Heart

> 「**怒则气逆，甚则呕血及飧泄，故气上矣。**」 ("In anger, qi counterflows; in severe cases, hematemesis and undigested-food diarrhea occur; thus qi rises.")

- In great anger, **qi surges up to attack the Heart**
- **Palpitations, chest oppression, heart pain**
- **In severe cases:** **myocardial infarction, cerebral hemorrhage** (**real-life cases**)

### 3. Injures the Spleen and Stomach

> 「**怒则气逆，甚则呕血及飧泄。**」 ("In anger, qi counterflows; in severe cases, hematemesis and undigested-food diarrhea occur.")

- In great anger, **liver qi horizontally counterflows to invade the Stomach (肝气横逆犯胃, gān qì héng nì fàn wèi)**
- **Stomach pain, vomiting, belching**
- **Indigestion, abdominal distension, diarrhea**
- In those with long-term repressed anger, **gastrointestinal function becomes disordered**

## II. Five Manifestations of Anger

### 1. Anger Outwardly Expressed

- **Characteristics:** flying into rage at the slightest thing, flushed face and red ears, loud rebukes
- **Consequences:** **injures qi, injures blood, injures Liver yang**
- **Commonly seen in:** patients with **liver yang hyperactivity**, **hypertension**, **hyperthyroidism**

### 2. Anger Inwardly Constrained

- **Characteristics:** angry but not expressing it, depressed and joyless, melancholic
- **Consequences:** **liver qi constraint (肝气郁结, gān qì yù jié)**, **qi stagnation with blood stasis (气滞血瘀, qì zhì xuè yū)**
- **Commonly seen in:** patients with **emotional depression**, **chronic gastritis**, **menstrual irregularity**

### 3. Anger Turning to Laughter

- **Characteristics:** anger so extreme it turns to laughter, forced smiles
- **Consequences:** **liver constraint transforming into fire**, **phlegm-fire harassing the Heart (痰火扰心, tán huǒ rǎo xīn)**
- **Commonly seen in:** those with **excessive emotional excitement**, **mental tension**

### 4. Anger Turning to Sorrow

- **Characteristics:** sorrow immediately following anger, regret and self-blame
- **Consequences:** **liver constraint with spleen deficiency (肝郁脾虚, gān yù pí xū)**, **dual deficiency of qi and blood (气血两虚, qì xuè liǎng xū)**
- **Commonly seen in:** **perfectionists**, those with **narcissistic personalities**

### 5. Anger Turning to Mania

- **Characteristics:** uncontrollable rage, destroying property, harming others
- **Consequences:** **internal stirring of liver wind**, **phlegm-fire clouding the Heart (痰火蒙心, tán huǒ méng xīn)**
- **Commonly seen in:** patients with **mania**, **alcoholism**, **severe mental illness**

## III. Eight Methods for Restraining Anger

### 🌿 Method One: Cognition — «Step Back and Open the Sky»

> 「**小不忍，则乱大谋。**」 ("Lack of forbearance in small things ruins great plans.") — *Lúnyǔ* (《论语》, Analects)

#### Think Thrice Before Anger

- **Step 1:** Before flying into rage, **take 3 deep breaths**
- **Step 2:** Ask yourself **«Will anger solve the problem?»**
- **Step 3:** Ask yourself **«What is the price of anger?»**
- **Step 4:** **Choose** not to be angry, or to **delay** anger

#### «The List of Costs»

Write down the **costs of your anger**:

- Harming your **body** (Liver, Heart, Spleen)
- Harming your **relationships** (family, friends, colleagues)
- Wasting **time** (arguing, repairing)
- Damaging your **image** (cultivation, bearing)

> One moment of anger: **a hundred harms and not a single benefit**.

### 🧘 Method Two: Breathing — «Abdominal Breathing Method»

#### Abdominal Breathing

- **Inhale:** **abdomen swells out**, imagine qi **entering through the nose** straight to the **dāntián (丹田, cinnabar field)**
- **Exhale:** **abdomen contracts**, imagine qi **exiting through the mouth**, carrying out **anger qi (怒气, nù qì)**
- **Frequency:** **6-8 times per minute**
- **Duration:** **3-5 minutes**

#### «6-Second Breathing Method»

- **Inhale 6 seconds**
- **Hold breath 2 seconds**
- **Exhale 6 seconds**
- **Hold breath 2 seconds**
- Repeat 5-10 times

> 「**呼吸之间，怒火自熄。**」 ("Between breaths, the fire of anger extinguishes itself.")

### 🌸 Method Three: Diversion — «Leave the Scene»

> 「**眼不见，心不烦。**」 ("Out of sight, out of mind.")

#### Of the Thirty-Six Stratagems, Retreat Is Best

- **Anger about to rise** — **leave the scene immediately**
- **Go for a walk** — go outdoors, into the open
- **Change the environment** — listen to music, read, take a bath
- **Do not** «confront on the spot»

#### «Time-Buffer Method»

- **Do not** be angry immediately
- **Wait 6 hours** (**or at least 1 hour**) before responding
- **90%** of «what one wanted to say on the spot» is **regretted afterward**

### 🤝 Method Four: Confiding — «Find Someone to Talk To»

> 「**情志之病，非药石可疗。**」 ("Diseases of emotion cannot be cured by drugs or stones.")

- **Find someone you trust** — family, friend, psychological counselor
- **Speak out the anger** — there is no need to bottle it up
- **Listen to the other's view** — perhaps **they are right**
- **Receive advice** — a single word may awaken the dreamer

#### «Three Friends»

- **One older than you** — deep in experience
- **One the same age as you** — strong in empathy
- **One younger than you** — fresh in perspective

### 🎨 Method Five: Release — «Healthy Discharge»

> 「**压抑怒气，不如合理发泄。**」 ("Repressing anger is worse than releasing it reasonably.")

#### Recommended Ways

- ✅ **Exercise:** running, boxing, swimming, fitness
- ✅ **Singing:** roar it out, sing high notes
- ✅ **Writing:** journaling, writing out emotions
- ✅ **Painting:** doodling freely
- ✅ **Punching a pillow:** strike in a safe place
- ✅ **Chopping vegetables:** chopping, kneading dough (without destroying things)
- ✅ **Tearing scrap paper:** tear newspapers, waste paper

#### Discouraged Ways

- ❌ **Cursing people** — harms others and self
- ❌ **Smashing things** — damages property
- ❌ **Self-abuse** — injures the body
- ❌ **Bingeing on food and drink** — injures the Spleen and Stomach
- ❌ **Heavy drinking** — injures the Liver
- ❌ **Driving** — angry driving is most dangerous

### 🌳 Method Six: Environment — «Get Close to Nature»

> 「**山林之中，怒气自消。**」 ("Among mountains and forests, anger dissipates on its own.")

- **At least once a week**, go into a **natural environment**
- **Mountains and forests:** course the Liver
- **Seaside:** broaden the heart
- **Grasslands:** open the horizons
- **Flower gardens:** cultivate the nature

#### «20 Minutes in the Park»

- **20 minutes daily** in a park or green space
- **No phone** — **look at the trees, grass, flowers, sky**
- **Deep breathing + slow walking**
- **Anger** will **naturally dissipate** in **nature's beauty**

### ☯ Method Eight: The Meridians — «Press Tàichōng, Tap the Gallbladder Meridian»

#### Tàichōng Point (太冲, LV-3)

- **Location:** on the dorsum of the foot, in the depression two finger-widths above the web between the great toe and the second toe
- **Technique:** **thumb press and knead**, **3-5 minutes per side**
- **Pressure:** **medium-strong**, **until a sore-distended sensation is reached (至酸胀为度, zhì suān zhàng wéi dù)**
- **Timing:** most effective **just as anger is rising**, or **just after anger has erupted**

> 「**太冲，肝经原穴，按之可解郁舒情。**」 ("Tàichōng, the source point (原穴, yuán xué) of the Liver Meridian — pressing it resolves depression and frees the emotions.")

#### Tap the Gallbladder Meridian (敲胆经, qiāo dǎn jīng)

- **Location:** outer side of the thigh, **at the trouser seam**
- **Technique:** tap with a **hollow fist**, **100-200 times per side**
- **Timing:** **1-2 times daily**

#### Qīmén Point (期门, LR-14)

- **Location:** on the chest, **directly below the nipple**, in the **6th intercostal space**
- **Technique:** **press and knead 2-3 minutes**
- **Effect:** **courses the Liver and regulates qi**

## IV. Daily Cultivation of Restraining Anger

### 📜 «Ten Precepts of Restraining Anger»

1. **Refrain from** «speaking carelessly in anger» — words that wound others, regrets that cannot be undone
2. **Refrain from** «refusing to leave your seat in anger» — get up and leave, **do not confront on the spot**
3. **Refrain from** «eating in anger» — in great anger, **do not** eat
4. **Refrain from** «driving in anger» — in great anger, **do not** drive
5. **Refrain from** «making decisions in anger» — in great anger, **do not** make decisions
6. **Refrain from** «scolding children in anger» — in great anger, **do not** discipline children
7. **Refrain from** «having intercourse in anger» — in great anger, **do not** have intercourse
8. **Refrain from** «drinking alcohol in anger» — in great anger, **do not** drink alcohol
9. **Refrain from** «being alone too long in anger» — in great anger, **do not** stay alone for too long
10. **Refrain from** «going straight to sleep in anger» — in great anger, **do not** immediately fall asleep

### 🌅 «Morning Self-Examination»

Each morning, ask yourself before the mirror:

> 「**今日，我可能因何事而怒？**」 ("Today, what might cause me anger?")

> 「**我当如何应对？**」 ("How should I respond?")

> 「**我能以何种心态处之？**」 ("With what mindset can I face it?")

> 「**我能从中学到什么？**」 ("What can I learn from it?")

### 🌙 «Evening Reflection»

Each evening before sleep, reflect:

> 「**今日，我可有怒气？**」 ("Today, did I have any anger?")

> 「**我处理得如何？**」 ("How well did I handle it?")

> 「**何处需改进？**」 ("Where can I improve?")

> 「**明日当如何？**」 ("What shall I do tomorrow?")

## V. Key Points of Restraining Anger for Different Groups

### 1. Young and Middle-Aged Adults

- **Stressors:** work, family, interpersonal relations
- **Characteristics:** easily **impatient and irritable**
- **Methods:** **exercise** + **confiding** + **nature** + **breathing**

### 2. Older Adults

- **Stressors:** health, children, loneliness
- **Characteristics:** easily **brood in anger, fume silently**
- **Methods:** **the four arts (qin, qi, calligraphy, painting)** + **socializing** + **diet** + **children's companionship**

### 3. Premenstrual Women

- **Characteristics:** 7-10 days before menstruation, **irritable and restless**
- **Methods:** **liver-coursing tea** + **Jiāwèi Xiāoyáo Wán (加味逍遥丸, Augmented Rambling Pill)** + **family understanding**

### 4. Menopausal Women

- **Characteristics:** **hot flashes, irritability, insomnia**
- **Methods:** **Gānmài Dàzǎo Tāng (甘麦大枣汤, Licorice, Wheat, and Jujube Decoction)** + **liver-coursing tea** + **exercise** + **psychological counseling**

### 5. Hypertensive Patients

- **Characteristics:** **blood pressure surges with anger**
- **Methods:** **absolute restraint of anger** + **antihypertensive medication** + **deep breathing** + **family cooperation**

## VI. When to See a Doctor

- ⚠️ **Frequent violent rage** that is **hard to control**
- ⚠️ **Harming people or destroying things after anger** (impulse control disorder)
- ⚠️ **Long-term repressed anger** leading to **severe depression, insomnia**
- ⚠️ **After great anger,** appearance of **chest pain, headache, vomiting, blurred vision** (**seek immediate medical attention**)

## Conclusion

> 「**百病皆生于气，养生首在节怒。**」 ("A hundred diseases all arise from qi; the first task of nourishing life is to restrain anger.")

No amount of Chinese yam or gǒuqǐ
can replenish the loss of a single fit of rage;
no amount of moxibustion or massage
can heal the hidden injuries of long-repressed anger.

**Restraining anger is the cheapest form of nourishing life;**
**restraining anger is the hardest of cultivations;**
**restraining anger is the greatest gift you can give your body.**

May you, from **today** —

- Before facing things, **think thrice** before anger
- Allow the fire of anger **six seconds**, then let it die down
- Each day **reflect** and improve

Let **anger qi** become **stillness qi**,
let **brooding anger** become **release and resolution**,
let **nourishing the Liver** begin with **restraining anger**.

**A lifetime of peace begins with «not getting angry»**.
