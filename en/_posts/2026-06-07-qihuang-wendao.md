> 「**Qíbó yuē:…… Shànggǔ zhī rén, qí zhīdào zhě, fǎ yú yīnyáng, hé yú shùshù — The people of high antiquity who knew the Way modeled themselves after Yin and Yang and harmonized with the arts of calculation.**」
>
> 「**Huángdì yuē: Shàn zāi! Yú wén shànggǔ yǒu zhēnrén zhě, tíqiè tiāndì, bǎwò yīnyáng — The Yellow Emperor said: Excellent! I have heard that in high antiquity there were true persons who grasped Heaven and Earth and held Yin and Yang in their hands.**」
>
> ——《Huángdì Nèijīng · Sùwèn · Shànggǔ Tiānzhēn Lùn》(*Huángdì Nèijīng* / *The Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon* · *Sùwèn* / *Basic Questions* · *Treatise on the True Nature of High Antiquity*)

**Qíhuáng (岐黄)** is the most ancient and most solemn epithet of Chinese medicine.
**Qí (岐)** stands for **Qíbó (岐伯)**; **Huáng (黄)** stands for **Huángdì (黄帝, the Yellow Emperor)**.
One was a **Son of Heaven of high antiquity**; the other was, in legend, **the Son of Heaven's teacher**.
They met in the **Míngtáng (明堂, the Hall of Brightness)** and asked and answered about the medical Way,
and from this encounter came **the "learning of Qíhuáng (岐黄之学)"**, **the "art of Qíhuáng (岐黄之术)"**,
and the saying that **"the one Way of Qíhuáng has been passed down for ten thousand generations without end (岐黄一道, 传之万世而不绝)"** — the **Way of Medicine (yīdào, 医道)**.

Where, then, did this **Way of Medicine (yīdào, 医道)** actually come from?
Having walked the **three-thousand-year** long road, what **roots** and **soul** has it left behind?

## I. Prologue: What Is "Qíhuáng"

> 「**Qí bó, Huáng dì chén yě. Dì shǐ Qí bó cháng wèi cǎomù, diǎn zhǔ yī bìng — Qíbó was a minister of the Yellow Emperor. The Emperor ordered Qíbó to taste the plants and to preside over the cure of disease.**」
>
> ——《Shǐjì · Bǔ Sānhóng Běnjì》(*Records of the Grand Historian · Supplement to the Annals of the Three August Ones*) (Táng dynasty · Sīmǎ Zhēn's supplement)

The two characters **"Qíhuáng (岐黄)"** — **Qí** denotes **Qíbó (岐伯)**, **Huáng** denotes **Huángdì (黄帝, the Yellow Emperor)**.
Most of the *Huángdì Nèijīng* is written in the format **"Huángdì asks, Qíbó answers"**,
so later generations simply called **Chinese Medicine** by the names **"Qíhuáng (岐黄)"**, **"the art of Qíhuáng (岐黄之术)"**, **"the Way of Qíhuáng (岐黄之道)"**.

**Qíhuáng** is the **"proper name"** of Chinese medicine;
**Qíhuáng Study (岐黄书房)** is the **little study** in which we, its guardians, tend this lineage of the **yīdào (医道, the Way of Medicine)**.

Today, beginning with the **remote-antique legend of Shénnóng's tasting of a hundred herbs**,
we shall walk along **the oracle bones of the Xià–Shāng–Zhōu dynasties, the renowned physicians of the Spring and Autumn and Warring States, and the canonical works of the Qín and Hàn dynasties**,
to see how this **three-thousand-year Way of Medicine** was, **step by step, laid in its foundations**.

## II. Remote Antiquity: The Sprouting of the Medical Way (Remote Antiquity — Shāng–Zhōu)

> 「**Shénnóng cháng bǎicǎo zhī zīwèi, yī rì ér yù qīshí dú — Shénnóng tasted the flavours of a hundred herbs, and in a single day encountered seventy poisons.**」
>
> ——《Huáinánzǐ · Xiūwù Xùn》(*Huáinánzǐ* / *The Master of Huáinán* · *Treatise on Diligent Cultivation*) (Western Hàn · Liu An)

### 🌿 Shénnóng Tastes a Hundred Herbs: The Source Stream of Materia Medica

In the high antiquity, were the **people free from disease and pestilence**? — Not so.
The *Huáinánzǐ · Xiūwù Xùn* states outright that Shénnóng **"in a single day encountered seventy poisons"**,
which shows that in that age **poisons and medicines existed side by side**,
and **tasting a hundred herbs** was an exploration **carried out at the risk of life itself**.

> 「**Shénnóng shì yǐ zhěbiān biān cǎomù, shǐ cháng bǎicǎo, shǐ yǒu yīyào — Shénnóng used a reddish whip to strike the plants, began to taste the hundred herbs, and so medicine began.**」
>
> ——《Shǐjì · Bǔ Sānhóng Běnjì》(*Records of the Grand Historian · Supplement to the Annals of the Three August Ones*)

The *Sōushénjì* (*In Search of the Supernatural*, Eastern Jìn · Gān Bǎo) likewise records:
**"Shénnóng, with a reddish whip, struck the hundred herbs, and came to know in full their natures of balance, poison, cold and warmth."**
This is the **fount of origin** of later **"materia medica studies (běncǎoxué, 本草学)"**.

> Encountering **seventy poisons in a single day** — this is not the **exaggeration of myth**,
> but the **solemn and stirring epic** of our forebears **trying medicines on their own bodies**, **exchanging life for medicine**.

### 📜 Fúxī Forges the Nine Needles: The Origin of Acupuncture

> 「**Fúxī shì nǎi cháng wèi bǎi yào ér zhì jiǔzhēn, yǐ zhěng yāowǎng yān — Fúxī tasted the hundred medicines and forged the Nine Needles, in order to rescue those cut off before their time.**」
>
> ——《Dìwáng Shìjì》(*Chronicle of the Emperors and Kings*) (Jìn · Huángfǔ Mì)

The **Nine Needles (jiǔzhēn, 九针)**, tradition says, were forged by **Fúxī (伏羲氏)** —
**the Chán needle (鑱针), the Yuán needle (员针), the Dī needle (鍉针), the Fēng needle (锋针), the Pī needle (铍针), the Yuánlì needle (员利针), the Háo needle (毫针), the Cháng needle (长针), and the Dà needle (大针)**,
nine differently shaped instruments, each matched to **nine different categories of illness**.
This is the earliest **legendary** origin of **needle therapy (zhēnciá liáofǎ, 针刺疗法)**,
later inherited and extended by the *Huángdì Nèijīng · Língshū* — which is why **the *Língshū*** is also called **"the Needle-Jing (Zhēnjīng, 针经)"**.

### 🦴 Shāng-dynasty Oracle Bones: The Earliest Records of Disease

Beyond **legend**, **empirical evidence** had also begun.
Among the **oracle-bone inscriptions (jiǎgǔ bǔcí, 甲骨卜辞)** of the **Shāng dynasty**,
a great many records concerning **"jí (疾, illness)"** have survived —
**"jí mù (疾目, eye-illness), jí ěr (疾耳, ear-illness), jí kǒu (疾口, mouth-illness), jí fù (疾腹, belly-illness), jí shēn (疾身, body-illness)"** …

Compiled by institutions such as the **China Academy of Chinese Medical Sciences (中国中医科学院)**,
the disease names legible in the oracle bones number in the **dozens**;
characters such as **"nüè (疟, malaria), jiè (疥, scabies), qǔ (龋, tooth-decay), gǔ (蛊, gu-disease)"** have all already appeared.
At the same time there are also the **shamanic prayers and divinations for "prayer that heals illness" (祈祷愈疾)** and **"warding off pestilence" (御疫)** —
which is the genuine face of an era in which **"shaman and physician were one (巫医一体)"**.

| Period | Character of the Medical Way | Main Records |
|---|---|---|
| Remote antiquity (legend) | Shénnóng tastes the hundred herbs / Fúxī forges the Nine Needles | *Huáinánzǐ*, *Sōushénjì*, *Dìwáng Shìjì* |
| Shāng dynasty (empirical) | Unity of shaman and physician; divining disease on oracle bones | Yīnxū oracle bones (c. 14th–11th c. BCE) |
| Western Zhōu | Specialisation of medical officials; emphasis on "food therapy (shíliáo, 食疗)" | *Zhōulǐ · Tiānguān* (Rites of Zhōu · Offices of Heaven) (Dietetician / Physician / Ulcer-Specialist / Veterinarian) |

> In this stage, **medicine** and **shamanism** had not yet fully parted;
> the **medical Way** was **slowly germinating** among **myth, magic and lived experience**.

## III. Spring and Autumn and Warring States: The Medical Way Splits Off (770–221 BCE)

In the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods, the **Hundred Schools contended**,
and the **medical Way** also began to **branch off from shamanism**,
producing **full-time physicians (zhuānyī, 专职医师)** and the **rudiments of the four examinations (sìzhěn, 四诊)**.

### 🧘 Yī Huǎn and Yī Hé: Two Famous Physicians of Qín

> 「**Gōng jíbìng, qiú yī yú Qín, Qín bó shǐ yī huǎn wèi zhī — Duke Jing fell ill and asked Qín for a physician; the Duke of Qín sent Physician Huǎn to attend him.**」
>
> ——《Zuǒzhuàn · Chénggōng Shí Nián》(*Zuǒ Tradition · Tenth Year of Duke Cheng*)

**Yī Huǎn (医缓)**, an excellent physician of the **state of Qín**, was once sent by **the Duke of Qín** to treat **Duke Jing of Jìn**,
and there gave rise to the chéngyǔ (idiom) **"the disease has entered gāo huāng (病入膏肓)"** —
**"the illness is beyond treatment; it lies above the huāng (the membrane) and below the gāo (the region of fat); to attack cannot reach it, to penetrate cannot get through, and medicine cannot arrive at the place"** (gāo huāng refers to the region between the heart and the diaphragm, a deep and inaccessible site).

**Yī Hé (医和)** went a step further, putting forward the famous **"six-qì doctrine of disease (liùqì zhìbìng shuō, 六气致病说)"**:
> 「**Tiān yǒu liùqì,…… yín shēng liù jí. Liùqì, yuē yīn, yáng, fēng, yǔ, huì, míng yě — Heaven has six qì,… from excess they generate six diseases. The six qì are called Yin, Yang, Wind, Rain, Darkness and Light.**」
>
> ——《Zuǒzhuàn · Zhāogōng Yuán Nián》(*Zuǒ Tradition · First Year of Duke Zhāo*)

This is the **earliest source** of the concept of the **"six excesses (liùyín, 六淫)"**,
and it pre-dates the *Huángdì Nèijīng*'s enumeration of **"wind, cold, summer-heat, damp, dryness, fire"** by **several hundred years**.

### 📜 Biǎn Què (Qín Yuèrén): The Founder of the Four Examinations

> 「**Biǎn Què zhě, Bóhǎi jùn Zhèng rén yě, xìng Qín shì, míng Yuèrén — Biǎn Què was a man of Zhèng in Bóhǎi Commandery, of the Qín surname, given name Yuèrén.**」
>
> ——《Shǐjì · Biǎn Què Cānggōng Lièzhuàn》(*Records of the Grand Historian · Biographies of Biǎn Què and Cāng Gōng*) (Western Hàn · Sīmǎ Qiǎn)

**Biǎn Què (扁鹊)** is **the first named physician in the history of Chinese medicine to have a formal biography**.
The *Shǐjì · Biǎn Què Cānggōng Lièzhuàn* records his **medical deeds in detail** —
**observing the complexion of Duke Huán of Qí (望齐桓侯之色)**, **diagnosing the Guó crown prince's corpse-like syncope (诊虢太子尸厥)**, **passing through Hándān to practise as a gynecological physician (过邯郸为带下医)** ……

The most famous episode is the story of **"hiding the illness and refusing the physician (huìjí jìyī, 讳疾忌医)"** in the *Hánfēizǐ · Yù Lǎo*:

> 「**Biǎn Què jiàn Cài Huán gōng, lì yǒu jiān. Biǎn Què yuē: Jūn yǒu jí zài còulǐ, bù zhì jiāng kǒng shēn.…… Jū shí rì, Biǎn Què wàng Huán hóu ér huán zǒu — Biǎn Què went to see Duke Huán of Cai, and stood for a while. Biǎn Què said: my lord, you have a sickness in the còulǐ (the fine texture of the skin); if untreated, I fear it will deepen.…… After ten days, Biǎn Quète glanced at Duke Huán from afar and turned back.**」

The reason Biǎn Què could **"know by looking (望而知之)"** lay in the foundation laid for the **"four examinations (sìzhěn, 四诊)"** of Chinese medicine —
**wàng (望, looking), wén (闻, listening and smelling), wèn (问, asking), qiè (切, pulse-taking)**.
He was honoured by later generations as **"the Ancestor of Pulse Studies (màixué zhī zǔ, 脉学之祖)"** and **"the Ancestor of Medical Prescriptions (yīfāng zhī zǔ, 医方之祖)"**.

### 🗣️ Wū Mǎqī: A Physician among the Disciples of Confucius

> 「**Wū Mǎqī yǐ xīng chū, yǐ xīng rù, rìyè bù chǔ yǐ shēn qīn zhī, ér shǐ Shànfǔ pì — Wū Mǎqī went out by starlight and came in by starlight, day and night not stopping, personally attending to all things, and so Shànfǔ came to be at peace.**」
>
> ——《Shuōyuàn · Zhènglǐ》(*Garden of Stories · Governing Principle*) (Western Hàn · Liú Xiàng, attributed also to *Hánshī Wàizhuàn*)

**Wū Mǎqī (巫马期)** (style name **Zǐqí (子旗)**, also written **"Shī (施)"**),
a **disciple of Confucius**, was once **Magistrate of Shànfǔ (单父, in present-day Shànxian, Shandong)**,
diligent in governance and caring for the people, **going out by starlight and returning by starlight (以星出, 以星入)**.
Later, the preface to the *Zhēnjiǔ Jiǎyǐ Jīng* (*The Systematic Classic of Acupuncture and Moxibustion*) also takes **"Wū Mǎqī"** as the representative of **Confucian scholars who understood medicine (zhī yī zhī rú zhě, 知医之儒者)**.

In this period the **medical Way** showed **two distinct threads**:
the one, represented by **Yī Huǎn of Qín, Yī Hé of Qín, and Qín Yuèrén (Biǎn Què)**, was the **professional physician**;
the other, represented by **Wū Mǎqī**, was the **germ of the Confucian physician (rúyī, 儒医)**.
The two **ran in parallel without conflict**, preparing the ground for the **great flourishing of the medical Way in the Qín and Hàn dynasties**.

## IV. The Qín and Hàn Dynasties: The Foundation of the Chinese-Medicine System (221 BCE – 220 CE)

> 「**Hàn xīng, gǎi Qín zhī bài, dà shōu piānjí, guǎng kāi xiànshū zhī lù — When the Hàn arose, it reformed the failures of the Qín, gathered writings on a great scale, and opened wide the road of presenting books.**」
>
> ——《Hànshū · Yìwén Zhì》(*Book of Hàn · Treatise on Literature and the Arts*) (Eastern Hàn · Bān Gù)

**The Qín and Hàn** are **the foundational age of the Chinese-medicine system**.
Four **immortal classics** — *Huángdì Nèijīng*, *Nánjīng*, *Shénnóng Běncǎo Jīng*, and *Shānghán Zábìng Lùn* —
were **completed one after another** within this millennium,
and **Chinese medicine** from then on possessed the four great pillars of a **complete theory, diagnostic method, materia medica, and formulary (lǐ-fǎ-fāng-yào, 理法方药)**.

### ☯ 1. *Huángdì Nèijīng* — The Fount-Head of Chinese-Medical Theory

The earliest **documentary mention** of the *Huángdì Nèijīng* is in the *Hànshū · Yìwén Zhì* of the Eastern-Hàn Bān Gù:
> 「**《Huángdì Nèijīng》 shíbā juǎn — The *Huángdì Nèijīng* is eighteen juǎn (fascicles).**」

Thereafter the **Sùwèn and the Língshū** are **9 juǎn each**, totalling **18 juǎn** and **162 chapters** (in the present edition: 81 chapters of the *Sùwèn* + 81 chapters of the *Língshū*) —
the **name "Nèijīng (Inner Canon)"** was fixed from this point on.

As to the **time of its composition**,
the **scholarly consensus** is:
> **It is not the work of any single person, at any single time, in any single place**,
> **it took shape between the Warring States and the Qín and Hàn**, and went through many rounds of **gathering, supplementing and revising**.

Its contents are **all-embracing**:
- **Yin-Yang and the five elements (yīnyáng wǔxíng, 阴阳五行)** — cosmology and methodology
- **Visceral manifestation and meridians (zàngxiàng jīngluò, 藏象经络)** — the structure of the human body and the channels of qì
- **Cause of disease and mechanism of disease (bìngyīn bìngjī, 病因病机)** — **"Where evils arise, they are born either in the Yin or in the Yang"** (《Língshū · Bǎi Bìng Shǐ Shēng》, *Língshū · The Generation of the Hundred Diseases*)
- **Diagnostic methods and therapeutic principles (zhěnfǎ zhìzé, 诊法治则)** — **"The skilled diagnostician examines the colour and presses the pulse, first distinguishing Yin from Yang"** (《Sùwèn · Yīnyáng Yìngxiàng Dàlùn》, *Sùwèn · The Great Treatise on the Correspondences of Yin and Yang*)
- **Nurturing life and prevention (yǎngshēng yùfáng, 养生预防)** — **"The superior physician treats the disease before it arises; he does not treat the disease once it has already arisen"** (《Língshū · Sìqì Tiáoshén Dàlùn*, *Língshū · The Great Treatise on the Regulation of the Spirit in the Four Qì*)

The **Nèijīng** is not the work of any single **"author"**,
but the **great compendium** of the **medicinal experience of the entire Chinese nation from high antiquity through the Qín and Hàn**.
The **"xiang sīwéi (image-thinking, 象思维)"** it established — using **Yin-Yang, the five elements, and refined qì (jīngqì, 精气)** as tools of reasoning,
and approaching life from the standpoint of **function, movement and holistic connection** —
is, even today, the **underlying logic of clinical Chinese medicine**.

> After the *Nèijīng*,
> the **"Way of Qíhuáng (岐黄之道)"** truly changed from **legend** into a **readable, learnable and transmissible classic**.

### 📜 2. *Nánjīng* — Supplementing What the *Nèijīng* Left Unsaid

> 「**《Huángdì Bāshíyī Nánjīng》 zhě, sī nǎi Bóhǎi Qín Yuèrén zhī suǒ zuò yě — The *Huángdì Bāshíyī Nánjīng* is the work of Qín Yuèrén of Bóhǎi.**」
>
> ——《Nánjīng Jízhù · Yáng Xuáncāo Xù》(*Collected Annotations to the Nánjīng · Preface by Yáng Xuáncāo*) (Táng · Yáng Xuáncāo)

The **Nánjīng** — full title **"Huángdì Bāshíyī Nánjīng" (《黄帝八十一难经》, The Yellow Emperor's Eighty-One Difficult Issues)** —
is attributed to **Biǎn Què (Qín Yuèrén)**, but was in fact composed in the **Eastern Hàn period**.
Written throughout in the **"question-and-difficulty (wènnàn, 问难)"** format, with **eighty-one questions**,
it **supplements the Nèijīng** in the areas of **pulse studies, meridians, needling methods, and visceral manifestation** —
and in particular it **systematises the method of "taking the cùnkǒu alone (dúqǔ cùnkǒu, 独取寸口)"** for pulse diagnosis,
laying the foundation for later **pulse studies (màixué, 脉学)**.

### 🌿 3. *Shénnóng Běncǎo Jīng* — The Foundational Work of Materia Medica

The **Shénnóng Běncǎo Jīng (《神农本草经》, The Divine Farmer's Classic of Materia Medica)** is **the earliest extant monograph on materia medica**,
completed in the **Eastern Hàn period** (some say the late Western Hàn),
attributed to **Shénnóng**, but in fact **compiled by physicians of successive generations**.

Its structure is extremely orderly:
- **365 medicinal substances are recorded** — "**corresponding to the number of the celestial circuit (yìng zhōutiān zhī shù, 应周天之数)**"
- **Divided into three grades — upper, middle and lower**:
  - **Upper grade: 120 substances** — "**They govern the nurturing of destiny and correspond to Heaven; they are non-toxic; taking them much and for long does not harm the person**" (examples: **rénshēn (ginseng, 人参), gāncǎo (licorice, 甘草), lóngyǎn (longan, 龙眼)**)
  - **Middle grade: 120 substances** — "**They govern the nurturing of nature and correspond to humans; some are non-toxic, some are toxic; use them with discernment**" (examples: **dāngguī (Chinese angelica, 当归), máhuáng (ephedra, 麻黄), sháoyào (peony, 芍药)**)
  - **Lower grade: 125 substances** — "**They govern the treatment of disease and correspond to Earth; they are mostly toxic and may not be taken for long**" (examples: **dàhuáng (rhubarb, 大黄), fùzǐ (aconite, 附子), bànxià (pinellia, 半夏)**)

Its skeleton —
**the four qì (sìqì, 四气: cold, hot, warm, cool), the five flavours (wǔwèi, 五味: sour, bitter, sweet, acrid, salty), the monarch–minister–assistant–envoy structure (jūnchén-zuǒshǐ, 君臣佐使), and the seven relations of combination (qīqíng, 七情)** —
**has not changed in two thousand years**,
and remains, even today, **the fundamental canon of clinical Chinese pharmacology**.

### 🧘 4. *Shānghán Zábìng Lùn* — The Clinical Cornerstone of Pattern Differentiation and Treatment

> 「**Yú zōngzú sù duō,…… gǎn wǎngxī zhī lúnsàng, shāng héngyāo zhī mò jiù, nǎi qínqiú gǔxùn, bócǎi zhòngfāng,…… wèi 《Shānghán Zábìng Lùn》 hé shíliù juǎn — My clan was originally numerous,…… moved by the fall of those who had passed away, grieved that those cut off in untimely death could not be rescued, I diligently sought the ancient teachings, broadly gathered the many prescriptions,…… and made the *Shānghán Zábìng Lùn* in sixteen juǎn.**」
>
> ——Eastern Hàn · Zhāng Zhòngjǐng's Preface to the *Shānghán Zábìng Lùn*

**Zhāng Zhòngjǐng (张仲景)** (c. 150–219 CE), a man of Nányáng in the **Eastern Hàn**,
was honoured by later generations as **"the Medical Sage (Yīshèng, 医圣)"**.

In the closing years of the Eastern Hàn, **great pestilences raged on every side** —
since the first year of the Jiàn'ān era (196 CE), **in less than ten years**, of his clan of **more than two hundred people**, **"two-thirds died, and of those, seven in ten died of cold-damage (shānghán, 伤寒)"** —
Zhòngjǐng, **"moved by the fall of those who had passed away, grieved that those cut off in untimely death could not be rescued"**,
**"diligently sought the ancient teachings and broadly gathered the many prescriptions"**,
and so composed the *Shānghán Zábìng Lùn* in sixteen juǎn.

> **Note**: Neither the *Hòu Hànshū* (*Book of the Later Hàn*) nor the *Sānguó Zhì* (*Records of the Three Kingdoms*) **erected a biography for Zhāng Zhòngjǐng**;
> the main sources for his life are later documents such as **the Preface to the *Zhēnjiǔ Jiǎyǐ Jīng* by Jìn-dynasty Huángfǔ Mì**, **the *Míngyī Lù* by Táng-dynasty Gān Bózōng**, and **Sòng-dynasty Lín Yì's collation of the *Shānghán Lùn***.

The greatest contribution of this book is the **creation of the clinical system of "pattern differentiation and treatment (biànzhèng lùnzhì, 辨证论治)"**:
- **Six-channel pattern differentiation (liùjīng biànzhèng, 六经辨证)** (Tàiyáng, Yángmíng, Shàoyáng, Tàiyīn, Shàoyīn, Juéyīn) — for **cold-damage (shānghán, 伤寒)**
- **Miscellaneous-disease treatment (zábìng lùnzhì, 杂病论治)** (zàng-fu and meridian pattern differentiation) — for the **Jīnkuì (金匮)**

It established the clinical principle **"observe the pulse and the pattern, perceive which reversal has been committed, and treat according to the pattern (观其脉证, 知犯何逆, 随证治之)"**,
and was honoured by later generations as **"the Ancestor of Prescriptions (fāngshū zhī zǔ, 方书之祖)"** —
the formulas it records, such as **Máhuáng Tāng (Ephedra Decoction), Guìzhī Tāng (Cinnamon Twig Decoction), Xiǎo Cháihú Tāng (Minor Bupleurum Decoction) and Wǔlíng Sǎn (Five-Ingredient Powder with Poria)**, are
**still in common clinical use in Chinese medicine today**.

> The four classics — one for **theory**, one for **pulse-taking**, one for **materia medica**, one for **prescriptions** —
> from this point on, **Chinese medicine had a complete system**, and **the Way of Qíhuáng** had **texts to rely upon and a learning to be passed down**.

## V. The Core Wisdom of the Way of Qíhuáng

Three thousand years of the medical Way have condensed, in the end, into **five** threads of **core wisdom**.
They are not **dead statutes**, but **a living soul**.

| Core Wisdom | Source in the Classics | Essence in One Sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **The holistic view (zhěngtǐ guān, 整体观)** | *Sùwèn · Yīnyáng Yìngxiàng Dàlùn* | A human being is a **whole**, and a human being together with **Heaven and Earth** is a **whole** |
| **Pattern differentiation and treatment (biànzhèng lùnzhì, 辨证论治)** | *Shānghán Lùn*, §16 | **"Observe the pulse and the pattern, perceive which reversal has been committed, and treat according to the pattern"** |
| **Treating the not-yet-manifest disease (zhì wèi bìng, 治未病)** | *Língshū · Sìqì Tiáoshén Dàlùn* | **"The superior physician treats the disease before it arises; he does not treat the disease once it has already arisen"** |
| **Heaven-human unity (tiānrén héyī, 天人合一)** | *Sùwèn · Bǎomìng Quánxíng Lùn* | **"Humans are born of the qì of Heaven and Earth, and made complete by the laws of the four seasons"** |
| **Yin-Yang and the five elements (yīnyáng wǔxíng, 阴阳五行)** | *Sùwèn · Yīnyáng Yìngxiàng Dàlùn* | **"Yin and Yang are the Way of Heaven and Earth, the great cord and net of the ten thousand things"** |

These **five** appear at first glance **mysterious**,
but in fact they are **the most plain and simple** of truths —
**treat the person as a person, treat disease as a process, and treat health as a responsibility**.

## VI. Why Chinese Medicine Has Endured Three Thousand Years Without Decline

> 「**Fán dà yī zhì bìng, bì dāng ānshén dìngzhì, wúyù wúqiú, xiān fā dàcí cèyǐn zhī xīn — Whenever a great physician treats disease, he must first calm his spirit and fix his intention; he must be without desire and without demand; he must first give rise to a heart of great compassion and pity.**」
>
> ——Táng-dynasty Sūn Sīmiǎo, *Bèijí Qiānjīn Yàofāng · Dàyī Jīngchéng* (*Thousand Golden Prescriptions for Urgent Need · The Essential Sincerity of the Great Physician*)

Through three thousand years of the medical Way, dynasties have risen and fallen, wars and tumults have been unceasing, and pestilences have been numberless;
yet **the art of Qíhuáng** has, again and again, **fallen and risen, transmitted without break**,
and its root lies in **four** points:

### 1. Clinical Effectiveness

Chinese medicine is **not** metaphysics, **not** a cultural symbol,
but a **medical system that truly cures disease**.
From **Máhuáng Tāng for external contractions**, **Xiǎo Cháihú Tāng to harmonise the Shàoyáng**,
to **acupuncture analgesia**, **tuī-ná (推拿, manual manipulation) for setting bones**, **moxibustion to warm the Yang** —
**thousands of years of human experimentation** have sifted out **countless effective formulas and therapies**.

In **2015**, the Chinese scientist **Tú Yōuyōu (屠呦呦)** was awarded the **Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine** (ceremony held 10 December 2015) for isolating **artemisinin (qīnghāosù, 青蒿素)** from the Chinese herb **qīnghāo (青蒿, *Artemisia annua*, sweet wormwood)**
in the treatment of **malaria**.
This is **a landmark contribution of Chinese medicine to world medicine** (according to the official Nobel Prize website).

### 2. A Complete Theoretical System

Unlike **folk empirical medicine**,
Chinese medicine, from the **Nèijīng** onward, has possessed **Yin-Yang, the five elements, visceral manifestation, the meridians, and the cause-and-mechanism of disease** —
a whole set of **deducible, verifiable and transmissible** **theoretical systems**.
Theory + clinic, **two tracks running in parallel** —
this is the key by which **Chinese medicine** was able to **step beyond the level of mere experience** and **arrive at the level of a discipline**.

### 3. Transmissibility

Chinese medicine has **two lines of transmission**:
- **Transmission through the texts** — word by word, character by character, **the torch passed from hand to hand**
- **Transmission through master and disciple** — one lineage, one pattern, **taught mouth to ear, taught heart to heart**

The **"master leading the disciple (shī dài tú, 师带徒)"** and **"reading the classics (dú jīngdiǎn, 读经典)"** taken together
have guaranteed the **living, active continuity (huótài yánxù, 活态延续)** of the **Way of Qíhuáng**.

### 4. Cultural Identity

> 「**Bù wéi liángxiàng, biàn wéi liángyī — If one cannot be a good prime minister, then one shall be a good physician.**」

This is the famous saying of **Fàn Zhòngyān (范仲淹)** of the **Northern Sòng** (recorded in the Sòng-dynasty Wú Zēng's *Nénggǎi Zhāi Mànlù*, j. 13),
and it is the **collective aspiration of the Chinese scholar-official class (shìrén, 士人)**.
Chinese medicine is **deeply embedded** within **Chinese culture** —
**Yin and Yang** come from the *Yì* (*Book of Changes*), the **five elements** from the *Shàngshū · Hóngfàn* (*Book of Documents · The Great Plan*),
**Heaven-human unity** from **the Hundred Schools of pre-Qín thought**.
It is not only **medicine**, but also **the world-view and methodology of the Chinese people**.

> According to the **ICD-11** (*International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems, Eleventh Revision*), adopted at the **72nd World Health Assembly in 2019** (World Health Organization),
> **"traditional medicine" was, for the first time, included**;
> the **internationalisation** of Chinese medicine and its **status in modern medicine** have been **formally recognised worldwide**.

## VII. Conclusion: The Medical Way Is Not Extinguished; Qíhuáng Shines On

> 「**Dà zāi qiánkūn, wúbèi dēnglín! Qíhuáng zhī dào, rìyuè gòngmíng — How vast are Heaven and Earth, that we have come to climb them! The Way of Qíhuáng shines as bright as sun and moon together.**」

Three thousand years is but **an instant in the long river of history**;
yet **the Way of Qíhuáng** has only **grown the fresher with time**.

From the **solemnity** of **Shénnóng tasting the hundred herbs**,
to the **wisdom** of **Yī Huǎn's discourse on gāo-huāng**;
from the **wonder** of **Biǎn Què's diagnosis by looking**,
to the **rigour** of **Zhòngjǐng's pattern differentiation**;
from the **great opening forth** of the **Nèijīng**,
to the **spirits dwelling in the plants** of the **Běncǎo** —
the **Way of Qíhuáng** has never been the wisdom of any one person,
but the **reverence for life**, the **compliance with nature**, the **guardianship of health**
of the **entire Chinese nation**.

> 「**Qí bó yuē:…… Shànggǔ zhī rén, qí zhīdào zhě, fǎ yú yīnyáng, hé yú shùshù, shíyǐn yǒu jié, qǐjū yǒu cháng, bù wàng zuò láo, gù néng xíng yǔ shén jù, ér jìn zhōng qí tiānnián, dù bǎisuì nǎi qù — Qíbó said:…… The people of high antiquity who knew the Way modeled themselves after Yin and Yang and harmonised with the arts of calculation; they ate and drank in measure, rose and rested with regularity; they did not tax themselves with useless toil. Therefore they were able to keep body and spirit together, and to fulfil the natural span of their years, passing the hundred years before they departed.**」
>
> ——《Huángdì Nèijīng · Sùwèn · Shànggǔ Tiānzhēn Lùn》(*Huángdì Nèijīng* / *The Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon* · *Sùwèn* / *Basic Questions* · *Treatise on the True Nature of High Antiquity*)

**Qíhuáng Study (岐黄书房)** is a **little study**,
and it wishes to be for you **a single lamp**, **a pot of tea**, **a stretch of text** —
so that in the **busy modern world**,
you may still **hear** the **echo of the medical Way** that comes from **three thousand years ago**:

> **Fǎ yú yīnyáng, hé yú shùshù —**
> **Model yourself on Yin and Yang; harmonise with the arts of calculation.**
>
> **Shíyǐn yǒu jié, qǐjū yǒu cháng —**
> **Eat and drink in measure; rise and rest in constancy.**
>
> **Xíng yǔ shén jù, ér jìn tiānnián —**
> **Body and spirit together, and so to fulfil the years of Heaven.**

**The medical Way is not extinguished; Qíhuáng shines on forever.**
**May we all be the torch-bearers of this *yīdào* (医道).**
