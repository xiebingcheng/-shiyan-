> 「**三阴交，在内踝上三寸，骨下陷者中，足太阴、少阴、厥阴之会。**」
>
> "Sānyīnjiāo lies three *cùn* above the medial malleolus, in the depression below the bone; it is the meeting point of the Foot-Taiyin (Spleen), Foot-Shaoyin (Kidney) and Foot-Jueyin (Liver) [meridians]."
>
> ——《Zhēnjiǔ Jiǎyǐ Jīng》(《针灸甲乙经》, *The Systematic Classic of Acupuncture and Moxibustion*)

Sānyīnjiāo (三阴交, Sānyīnjiāo, SP-6) is **a point of the Foot-Taiyin Spleen Meridian (足太阴脾经)**.
Why is it called "Sānyīnjiāo" (Three Yin Intersection)?
Because it is the **meeting place (交会之处) of three yin meridians (三阴经) — the Foot-Taiyin Spleen Meridian (足太阴脾经), the Foot-Shaoyin Kidney Meridian (足少阴肾经), and the Foot-Jueyin Liver Meridian (足厥阴肝经)**.

One point reaches through three meridians; three meridians regulate the whole body.
**The foremost gynecological point (妇科第一穴)**, **the foremost calming-spirit point (安神第一穴)**, **an essential point for regulating the Spleen and Stomach (脾胃调摄要穴)** — Sānyīnjiāo lives up to its name.

## I. Location of Sānyīnjiāo

### A Simple Method to Find the Point

1. **Step one:** **sit or lie supine**, with the lower leg slightly adducted
2. **Step two:** find the **medial malleolus (内踝尖)**
3. **Step three:** measure **four finger-widths (tóng shēn cùn, 同身寸 3 *cùn*) upward from the tip of the medial malleolus**
4. **Step four:** the **posterior border of the medial surface of the tibia (胫骨内后缘)** is the point

### Precise Location

- On the **inner side of the lower leg**, 3 *cùn* (tóng shēn cùn, 同身寸) above the tip of the medial malleolus
- On the **posterior border of the medial surface of the tibia (胫骨内侧面后缘)**
- Between the **tibialis posterior tendon (胫骨后肌腱)** and the **flexor digitorum longus (趾长屈肌)**
- There lies the **great saphenous vein (大隐静脉)**, and the **medial sural cutaneous nerve (小腿内侧皮神经)** and **tibial nerve (胫神经)** are distributed there

> Note: do not confuse it with **Xuánzhōng (悬钟, GB-39)** (3 *cùn* above the lateral malleolus, lateral to the tibia) — **absolutely never confuse them**.

## II. The Nature of Sānyīnjiāo

### 1. Meridian Attribution

- Belongs to the **Foot-Taiyin Spleen Meridian (足太阴脾经)**
- Is the **meeting point (交会穴) of the three foot-yin meridians (足三阴经) — Spleen, Liver, Kidney**

### 2. Effects

- **Fortifies the Spleen (健脾)** — boosts qi and nourishes blood, transforms dampness and leaches water
- **Tonifies the Kidney (补肾)** — nourishes kidney yin, secures essence and stops leukorrhea
- **Soothes the Liver (疏肝)** — regulates qi movement, resolves constraint and depression
- **Regulates menstruation (调经)** — regulates the chong and ren vessels, activates blood and stops pain
- **Calms the spirit (安神)** — connects Heart and Kidney, draws yang into yin

### 3. Point Nature

- Inclined toward **tonification (补益)** — the qi of the three yin meridians is all yin blood, so Sānyīnjiāo's function of **enriching yin and nourishing blood (滋阴养血) is the strongest**
- Inclined toward **downward action (下行)** — draws blood downward, returns fire to its source, draws yang into yin
- Inclined toward **regulation (调摄)** — regulates three zang, three meridians, the yin of the whole body

## III. The Principle of Sānyīnjiāo in Treating Insomnia

### The Pathogenesis of Insomnia

In Chinese medicine, insomnia can be summarized in four characters: **"yáng does not enter yīn" (阳不入阴)**.
Specifically:

- **Heart-Kidney non-communication (心肾不交)** — heart fire (心火) flares, kidney water (肾水) is deficient, the spirit cannot remain in its abode
- **Heart-Spleen dual deficiency (心脾两虚)** — qi and blood are insufficient, the Heart spirit is not nourished
- **Liver constraint transforming into fire (肝郁化火)** — liver fire disturbs the heart, the ethereal soul (魂, hún) does not rest
- **Phlegm-heat internal disturbance (痰热内扰)** — phlegm-heat clouds the heart, the spirit is unquiet
- **Heart-Gallbladder qi deficiency (心胆气虚)** — both Heart and Gallbladder are timid, easily startled and easily awoken

### How Sānyīnjiāo Treats Insomnia

The **three meridians of Liver, Spleen and Kidney** have the closest relationship with the **Heart (心)**:

- **Spleen (脾)**: the source of qi and blood transformation, **transforms blood to nourish the Heart (化血以养心)**
- **Liver (肝)**: stores blood, **blood houses the ethereal soul (魂, hún)**, when the soul is at peace, sleep is sound
- **Kidney (肾)**: stores essence, **essence transforms into marrow (精化髓)** and ascends to the brain; when the brain marrow is full, the spirit is at peace

Sānyīnjiāo **simultaneously harmonizes the three meridians of Liver, Spleen and Kidney**,
so that **qi and blood are abundant, the ethereal and corporeal souls (魂魄) are at peace, the Heart spirit is nourished, yin is balanced and yang is in repose** (阴平阳秘),
and insomnia removes itself.

> 「**阳气自动而之静，则寐；阳气自静而之动，则寤。**」 ("When yang qi goes of itself from movement to stillness, sleep comes; when yang qi goes of itself from stillness to movement, one wakes.")
> Sānyīnjiāo **draws yang into yin** — it is a key link in the treatment of insomnia.

## IV. Specific Methods of Sānyīnjiāo for Calming Sleep

### 1. Pressing and Kneading Method (**the safest**)

- **Timing:** **1 hour before sleep**
- **Technique:** **thumb pad (拇指) presses**, **from light to heavy**
- **Force:** **moderately heavy, to the degree of soreness and distension (以酸胀为度)**
- **Duration:** **5-10 minutes per side**
- **Direction:** **clockwise (顺时针) is tonifying**, **counter-clockwise (逆时针) is draining**
- **For insomnia, use tonification more** — knead clockwise

**Reinforcing techniques:**

- After kneading, **pat Sānyīnjiāo** 100 times
- Or **warm both palms by rubbing**, then knead Sānyīnjiāo for 3 minutes

### 2. Moxibustion Method (**most effective for deficiency-type insomnia**)

- **Method:** **mild-warm moxibustion (温和灸)**
- **Duration:** **15-20 minutes per side**
- **Timing:** **1-2 hours before sleep** is best
- **Course:** **once daily, 10 sessions constitute one course**

**Advantages of moxibustion at Sānyīnjiāo:**

- Warms and unblocks the three yin, **returns fire to its source (引火归元)**
- Harmonizes qi and blood, **nourishes the Heart and calms the spirit (养心安神)**
- Suitable for **Heart-Spleen dual deficiency, Liver constraint with blood deficiency, Heart-Kidney non-communication** types of insomnia

**Special note:** for those with **yin deficiency with effulgent fire (阴虚火旺), dry mouth and tongue, red tongue with scant coating**, **reduce moxibustion dose (within 10 minutes)** to avoid assisting fire.

### 3. Acupoint Sticking Method

**Wángbùliúxíng seed ear-point plasters (王不留行籽耳穴贴)** (available at hospitals or pharmacies):

- Select **Shénmén (神门), Pízhìxià (皮质下), Heart (心), Kidney (肾), Liver (肝), Spleen (脾)** and other ear points
- Press **3-5 times daily**, **1-2 minutes per point**
- **Change every 3-5 days**, alternating left and right ears

**Body-point application (自制的, homemade):**

- Mix **Wúzhūyú (吴茱萸, Evodia rutaecarpa) powder** with **rice vinegar (米醋)** into a paste
- Before bed at night, apply to **Yǒngquán (涌泉, KD-1)** (the depression in the anterior 1/3 of the sole)
- Remove in the morning
- **Returns fire to its source**, especially suitable for **Heart-Kidney non-communication** type insomnia

### 4. Hot Water Foot-Bath Method

- **Every night before bed**, soak the feet in **40-45 °C hot water**
- Water up to **the middle of the lower leg** (the **Sānyīnjiāo location**)
- **Soak for 20-30 minutes**
- **Ginger slices, Sichuan pepper, and mugwort leaves (生姜片、花椒、艾叶)** may be added to the water
- Stop when **a slight sweat appears**

> 「**春天洗脚，升阳固脱；夏天洗脚，暑湿可祛；**
> **秋天洗脚，肺润肠濡；冬天洗脚，丹田温灼。**」
> ("Washing the feet in spring raises yang and prevents collapse; in summer removes summer-damp; in autumn moistens the Lungs and intestines; in winter warms the dāntián.")

Soaking the feet in hot water can **warm and unblock the three yin meridians, accelerate the movement of qi and blood, and draw yang into yin**,
making it the most **simple and easy** method of calming sleep.

## V. Acupoint Pairings for Different Patterns of Insomnia

### 1. Heart-Spleen Dual Deficiency Insomnia (心脾两虚型)

- **Features:** difficulty falling asleep, dream-disturbed sleep with easy waking, fatigue and forgetfulness
- **Tongue and pulse:** pale tongue with white coating, thin and weak pulse
- **Main points:** **Sānyīnjiāo (三阴交)** + **Shénmén (神门, HT-7)** + **Xīnshù (心俞, BL-15)** + **Píshù (脾俞, BL-20)**

### 2. Liver Constraint Transforming into Fire Insomnia (肝郁化火型)

- **Features:** difficulty falling asleep, irritability and rage, bitter mouth and red eyes
- **Tongue and pulse:** red tongue with yellow coating, wiry and rapid pulse
- **Main points:** **Sānyīnjiāo (三阴交)** + **Tàichōng (太冲, LR-3)** + **Xíngjiān (行间, LR-2)** + **Shénmén (神门, HT-7)**

### 3. Heart-Kidney Non-Communication Insomnia (心肾不交型)

- **Features:** vexation with tinnitus, soreness and weakness of the lower back and knees, vexing heat in the five centers (palms, soles, chest)
- **Tongue and pulse:** red tongue with scant coating, thin and rapid pulse
- **Main points:** **Sānyīnjiāo (三阴交)** + **Yǒngquán (涌泉, KD-1)** + **Shénmén (神门, HT-7)** + **Tàixī (太溪, KD-3)**

### 4. Phlegm-Heat Internal Disturbance Insomnia (痰热内扰型)

- **Features:** vexation with sleeplessness, chest oppression and dizziness, profuse phlegm and bitter mouth
- **Tongue and pulse:** red tongue with yellow greasy coating, slippery and rapid pulse
- **Main points:** **Sānyīnjiāo (三阴交)** + **Fēnglóng (丰隆, ST-40)** + **Nèitíng (内庭, ST-44)** + **Shénmén (神门, HT-7)**

### 5. Heart-Gallbladder Qi Deficiency Insomnia (心胆气虚型)

- **Features:** easily startled and easily awoken, palpitations and timidity, profuse nightmares
- **Tongue and pulse:** pale tongue with thin white coating, wiry and thin pulse
- **Main points:** **Sānyīnjiāo (三阴交)** + **Shénmén (神门, HT-7)** + **Dǎnshù (胆俞, BL-19)** + **Qiūxū (丘墟, GB-40)**

## VI. Other Marvelous Uses of Sānyīnjiāo

### 1. The Foremost Gynecological Point

> 「**妇科三阴交。**」 ("For gynecology, Sānyīnjiāo.") — a gynecological acupuncture verse

**Menstrual irregularity, dysmenorrhea, amenorrhea, leukorrhea, uterine fibroids, ovarian cysts, infertility** — Sānyīnjiāo is **almost always required in gynecology**.

- **Dysmenorrhea:** start pressing Sānyīnjiāo **3-5 days before menstruation**, **twice daily**, **5 minutes per side**
- **Menstrual irregularity:** Sānyīnjiāo + **Guānyuán (关元, CV-4)** + **Qìhǎi (气海, CV-6)** + **Xuèhǎi (血海, SP-10)**
- **Profuse leukorrhea:** Sānyīnjiāo + **Dàimài (带脉, GB-26)** + **Zúlínqì (足临泣, GB-41)**
- **Infertility:** Sānyīnjiāo + **Guānyuán (关元, CV-4)** + **Zǐgōng (子宫, EX-CA1)** + **Shènshù (肾俞, BL-23)**

### 2. An Essential Men's Health Point

- **Impotence, seminal emission, premature ejaculation:** Sānyīnjiāo + **Guānyuán (关元, CV-4)** + **Shènshù (肾俞, BL-23)** + **Mìngmén (命门, GV-4)**
- **Prostatitis:** Sānyīnjiāo + **Zhōngjí (中极, CV-3)** + **Yīnlíngquán (阴陵泉, SP-9)**

### 3. Regulation of Spleen and Stomach

- **Abdominal distension, loose stools, poor appetite:** Sānyīnjiāo + **Zúsānlǐ (足三里, ST-36)** + **Zhōngwǎn (中脘, CV-12)**
- **Edema, dysuria:** Sānyīnjiāo + **Yīnlíngquán (阴陵泉, SP-9)** + **Shuǐfèn (水分, CV-9)**

### 4. Skin Disorders

> 「**治风先治血，血行风自灭。**」 ("To treat wind, first treat blood; when blood moves, wind extinguishes of itself.")

Sānyīnjiāo is **an essential point for nourishing blood**,
and has good effects on **chronic urticaria, eczema, and skin itching (慢性荨麻疹、湿疹、皮肤瘙痒)**.

## VII. Contraindications for Sānyīnjiāo

### ❌ 1. **Absolutely contraindicated** for needle in pregnancy

> 「**孕妇禁针合谷、三阴交。**」 ("Pregnant women must not be needled at Hégǔ (LI-4) or Sānyīnjiāo (SP-6).")

**Needling Sānyīnjiāo in pregnancy** may **disturb the fetal qi and cause miscarriage**.
But can **pregnant women** **press or moxibust Sānyīnjiāo**?

- **Early pregnancy (first 3 months):** **forbidden** to press hard or moxibust strongly
- **Mid pregnancy (4-7 months):** **light pressing** is allowed; **heavy pressing or strong moxibustion** requires physician guidance
- **Late pregnancy (after 8 months):** **light pressing of Sānyīnjiāo** is allowed, **helpful for natural delivery**

### ❌ 2. **Use with caution** during menstruation

During menstruation, **heavy pressing or strong moxibustion** of Sānyīnjiāo may **activate blood too strongly**,
causing excessive menstrual bleeding.
For those with **normal menstrual volume**, **light pressing** is allowed; for those with **heavy menstrual volume**, **pressing is forbidden**.

### ❌ 3. **Use with caution** in excess and heat patterns

Sānyīnjiāo is a **tonification point**, inclined toward tonifying;
for **excess heat or exuberant evil qi**, **strong moxibustion** may **assist fire**.

### ❌ 4. **Moxibustion forbidden** when local skin is abnormal

When the skin at Sānyīnjiāo has **breakdown, ulceration, or sores (皮肤破损、溃疡、疮疡)**, moxibustion is forbidden.
