> 「**百病生于气，止于音。**」
>
> 「**宫动脾，商动肺，角动肝，徵动心，羽动肾。**」
>
> — *Huángdì Nèijīng* (《黄帝内经》, Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon), *Sùwèn* (《素问》, Basic Questions), *Yīnyáng Yìngxiàng Dàlùn* (《阴阳应象大论》, Great Treatise on the Correspondences of Yin and Yang)

TCM holds that the **five notes (五音: jué (角), zhǐ (徵), gōng (宫), shāng (商), yǔ (羽))** correspond to the **five elements (五行: Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water)**,
and correspond to the **five zang (五脏: Liver, Heart, Spleen, Lung, Kidney)**.

> 「**天有五音，人有五脏；天有六律，人有六腑。**」 ("Heaven has five notes, man has five zang; Heaven has six pitches, man has six fu.")

**Music is not only an art — it is medicine.**
Listening to the right music can **course the Liver, nourish the Heart, fortify the Spleen, moisten the Lungs, and tonify the Kidneys**;
listening to the wrong music may **injure the emotions, stir the qi, and damage the zang**.

## I. The Correspondence of the Five Notes and the Five Zang

### What Are the "Five Notes" (五音)?

Traditional Chinese music uses the **"gōng, shāng, jué, zhǐ, yǔ" (宫、商、角、徵、羽)** pentatonic scale,
corresponding to the numbered notation **1 (do), 2 (re), 3 (mi), 5 (sol), 6 (la)**.

| Five notes | Sung name | Numbered notation | Equivalent in Western solfège |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Jué (角)** | jué | mi | 3 |
| **Zhǐ (徵)** | zhǐ | sol | 5 |
| **Gōng (宫)** | gōng | do | 1 |
| **Shāng (商)** | shāng | re | 2 |
| **Yǔ (羽)** | yǔ | la | 6 |

### The Five Elements Paired with the Five Zang

| Five notes | Five elements | Five zang | Five fu | Five emotions | Five seasons | Five colors |
|------------|---------------|-----------|---------|---------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Jué (角)** | Wood | **Liver (肝)** | Gallbladder | Anger | Spring | Green/blue |
| **Zhǐ (徵)** | Fire | **Heart (心)** | Small intestine | Joy | Summer | Red |
| **Gōng (宫)** | Earth | **Spleen (脾)** | Stomach | Overthinking | Late summer | Yellow |
| **Shāng (商)** | Metal | **Lung (肺)** | Large intestine | Grief | Autumn | White |
| **Yǔ (羽)** | Water | **Kidney (肾)** | Bladder | Fear | Winter | Black |

### The Ancient Teaching of "Five Notes Treating Disease"

## II. The Principles of Five-Note Therapy

### 1. Resonance of Like with Like (同气相求)

- **Jué notes (角, mi)** — Wood-natured, unblocking; can **course the Liver and resolve depression (疏肝解郁)**
- **Zhǐ notes (徵, sol)** — Fire-natured, flaring upward; can **nourish the Heart and quieten the spirit (养心安神)**
- **Gōng notes (宫, do)** — Earth-natured, honest and generous; can **fortify the Spleen and harmonize the Stomach (健脾和胃)**
- **Shāng notes (商, re)** — Metal-natured, clear and pure; can **moisten the Lungs and stop cough (润肺止咳)**
- **Yǔ notes (羽, la)** — Water-natured, cold and moist; can **tonify the Kidneys and secure essence (补肾固精)**

- ✅ Relieve **anxiety, depression, and insomnia (焦虑、抑郁、失眠)**
- ✅ Regulate **immune function (免疫功能)**
- ✅ Reduce **pain (疼痛)**

### 3. Clinical Applications

The TCM **Five-Note Therapy (五音疗法)** has been used for:

- Adjunct treatment of anxiety and depression
- Regulation of insomnia
- Relief of chronic pain
- Psychological rehabilitation of tumor patients
- Relief of perioperative anxiety
- Health-preservation care for healthy people

## III. Representative Pieces for Each Zang

### 🎵 1. Jué (角) Note — Courses the Liver — The Note of "Wood"

**Main melody:** pieces with **mi** as the tonic
**Style:** **fresh, expansive, unobstructed, melodious (清新、舒展、条达、悠扬)**
**Recommended pieces:**

- **«Eighteen Songs of a Nomad Flute» (《胡笳十八拍》)** — by Cai Wenji, **the foremost piece for coursing the Liver and resolving depression (疏肝解郁第一曲)**
- **«A Moonlit Night on the Spring River» (《春江花月夜》)** — spring night, river, and moon, **courses the Liver and nourishes the Heart (疏肝又养心)**
- **«Jiangnan Silk and Bamboo» (《江南丝竹》)** — graceful and tender, **most suited to spring (最宜春日)**
- **«Sunny Spring and White Snow» (《阳春白雪》)** — fresh and bright, **courses the Liver and fortifies the Spleen (疏肝健脾)**
- **«A Trip to Suzhou» (《姑苏行》)** — springtime in Jiangnan, **courses the Liver and harmonizes the Stomach (疏肝和胃)**

**Suitable for:**

- Those with emotional depression
- Those with chest and flank distension and pain
- Those with dizziness and vertigo
- Women with menstrual irregularity (liver-constraint type)

**Contraindications:**

- ❌ Those with **liver yang hyperactivity (肝阳上亢, liver yang hyperactivity)** (hypertension, dizziness, flushed face)
- ❌ Those with **liver fire effulgence (肝火旺)** (bitter mouth, irritability, red eyes)

### 🎵 2. Zhǐ (徵) Note — Nourishes the Heart — The Note of "Fire"

**Main melody:** pieces with **sol** as the tonic
**Style:** **warm, joyful, flowing, passionate (热烈、欢快、流畅、激昂)**
**Recommended pieces:**

- **«Purple Bamboo Melody» (《紫竹调》)** — Jiangnan folk song, **nourishes the Heart and quiets the spirit (养心安神)**
- **«Flowers in Bloom, Full Moon» (《花好月圆》)** — festive and warm, **nourishes the Heart and delights the spirit (养心悦神)**
- **«A Hundred Birds Paying Homage to the Phoenix» (《百鸟朝凤》)** — a suona masterwork, **raises Heart yang (提振心阳)**
- **«Horse Race» (《赛马》)** — a erhu masterwork, **invigorates Heart qi (振奋心气)**
- **«Rising Step by Step» (《步步高》)** — an ethnic piece, **inspires Heart spirit (鼓舞心志)**

**Suitable for:**

**Contraindications:**

- ❌ Those with **heart fire effulgence (心火亢盛)** (red tongue tip, mouth and tongue sores)
- ❌ Those in states of excitement and agitation (not suited before sleep)

**Style:** **honest, solemn, calm, gentle (敦厚、庄重、沉静、舒缓)**
**Recommended pieces:**

## IV. The Specific Application of Five-Notes to Course the Liver

### 1. Spring Health — Listen Often to Jué (角) Note

> 「**春三月，此谓发陈……此春气之应，养生之道也。**」

In spring, **Liver qi is in command**, and emotions easily become depressed, easily become angry.
**Listening 30 minutes a day to jué notes** can:

- ✅ Course the Liver and resolve depression
- ✅ Smooth the qi movement
- ✅ Lift the emotions
- ✅ Help yang rise and generate

**Recommended:** listen to «Eighteen Songs of a Nomad Flute» (《胡笳十八拍》), «A Moonlit Night on the Spring River» (《春江花月夜》), «Sunny Spring and White Snow» (《阳春白雪》) on rising, at noon, and while walking.

### 2. Emotional Depression — Listen Often to Jué (角) and Gōng (宫) Notes

- Those with depression mostly have **liver qi constraint (肝气郁结)** and **spleen failing to transport (脾失健运)**
- Jué notes course the Liver + gōng notes fortify the Spleen = 「**course the Liver and fortify the Liver (疏肝健脾)**」 *(sic — in original)*
- Combined with **outdoor activity** and **exercise**, the effect is even better

### 3. Hypertension (Liver Yang Hyperactivity, 肝阳上亢) — Listen Often to Yǔ (羽) and Jué (角) Notes

- Hypertension is mostly caused by **liver yang hyperactivity (肝阳上亢)**
- **Yǔ notes (la)** — enrich yin and subdue yang, tonify the Kidneys and descend fire
- **Jué notes (mi)** — course the Liver, pacify the Liver
- Combined with **low-pitched, gentle** music
- Avoid **zhǐ notes and passionate** pieces

### 4. Insomnia — Listen Often to Yǔ (羽) and Gōng (宫) Notes

- Insomnia is mostly **Heart-Kidney non-communication (心肾不交)** and **heart fire effulgence (心火亢盛)**
- **Yǔ notes (la)** — tonify the Kidneys, return fire to its source
- **Gōng notes (do)** — fortify the Spleen, quiet the Heart
- Combined with **low-pitched, slow-tempo** pieces
- Listening 1 hour before sleep is even more effective

## V. Specific Operations of Five-Note Therapy

### 🎧 1. Listening Therapy (听音疗法)

- **Equipment:** loudspeakers or headphones, both fine
- **Volume:** **40-60 decibels** is appropriate (moderate-low)
- **Duration:** **30 minutes per session** is best
- **Frequency:** **1-2 times daily**
- **Environment:** **quiet, comfortable, relaxed**

### 🎤 2. Singing Therapy (唱音疗法) (**more effective**)

> 「**歌咏可以养心。**」 ("Singing can nourish the Heart.")

- **Singing (唱)** the five notes oneself is more effective than merely **listening (听)**
- One may choose the **Five-Note Song (五音歌)** (recorded in the *Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon*), or sing melodies based on jué, zhǐ, gōng, shāng, yǔ
- **1-2 times daily**, **15-20 minutes each time**
- Suitable for those with **lung qi deficiency** and **emotional depression**

### 🎵 3. Performance Therapy (演奏疗法)

- **Performing (演奏)** an instrument oneself (guqin, xiao, dizi, erhu, etc.)
- Suitable for those with a musical background
- Effect is deeper than mere listening

### 🧘 4. Music Daoyin (音乐导引)

- While listening, **combine with breathing, meditation, and daoyin (导引)**
- **Inhale (吸气)** imagining the music **entering the zang-fu**
- **Exhale (呼气)** imagining **pathogenic qi (病气)** being expelled
- Suitable for **qigong (气功) enthusiasts**

## VI. Precautions for Five-Note Therapy

### ✅ 1. Choose Pieces According to Person, Time, and Place

- **According to the person (因人):** based on **constitution and pattern (体质、证型)**
- **According to the time (因时):** based on **season and hour (季节、时辰)**
- **According to the place (因地):** based on **environment and mood (环境、心情)**

### ✅ 2. Volume Should Be Moderate

- ❌ **Volume too loud** — injures the ears, stirs the qi
- ❌ **Volume too soft** — hard to resonate
- ✅ **40-60 decibels** is most appropriate

### ✅ 3. Duration Should Be Appropriate

- ❌ **Time too long** — injures qi and spirit
- ❌ **Time too short** — hard to take effect
- ✅ **30-60 minutes** is most appropriate

### ✅ 4. Note the Contraindications

- **When emotionally aroused** — **not suited** to **zhǐ notes or passionate** pieces
- **Before sleep** — **not suited** to **high-pitched, passionate** pieces
- **Liver yang hyperactivity (肝阳上亢)** — **not suited** to **jué or zhǐ notes**
- **Lung-heat cough (肺热咳嗽)** — **not suited** to **shāng notes or mournful** pieces

### ✅ 5. Combine with Other Therapies

- Five-Note Therapy is an **adjunct** therapy and **cannot replace** medical treatment
- **Serious diseases** (such as mental illness, severe insomnia) **require medical care**
- Five notes + **diet** + **daily routine** + **exercise** + **emotions** = **complete yangsheng (养生)**

## VII. Five Notes and Modern Music

The TCM five notes correspond to modern modes as follows:

| Five notes | Tonic | Mode | Modern key signatures |
|------------|-------|------|------------------------|
| Jué (角) | mi | E mode | E gōng, E shāng, E jué, E zhǐ, E yǔ |
| Zhǐ (徵) | sol | G mode | Various G-mode pieces |
| Gōng (宫) | do | C mode | Various C-mode pieces |
| Shāng (商) | re | D mode | Various D-mode pieces |
| Yǔ (羽) | la | A mode | Various A-mode pieces |

In fact, **a complete piece of music** is often **multi-modal (多调式)**;
the key lies in **which note (主音) is used as the tonic of the main melody**,
and the **overall sense (整体调性)**:

- **Fresh and expansive (清新舒展)** → jué note
- **Passionate and joyful (热烈欢快)** → zhǐ note
- **Honest and solemn (敦厚庄重)** → gōng note
- **High and mournful (高亢悲切)** → shāng note
- **Low and far-reaching (低沉悠远)** → yǔ note
