> 「**清明断雪，谷雨断霜。**」
>
> 「**谷雨春光晓，山川黛色青。**」
>
> "Qīngmíng severs snow, Gǔyǔ severs frost."
>
> "Gǔyǔ's spring light dawns; mountains and rivers, indigo and green."

Gǔyǔ (谷雨, Gǔyǔ, Grain Rain) is **the last solar term of spring**.
The *Yuèlìng Qīshí'èr Hòu Jíjiě* (《月令七十二候集解》, Collection of Explanations of the Seventy-Two Pentads of the Monthly Ordinances) says: "Midway through the third month: after Yǔshuǐ (雨水, Rain Water) the earth's vital channels move, and now rain falls upon the grain in the water… for the grain is sown at this time, from top to bottom."

The grain receives the rain and is born; **spring rain is as precious as oil**.
This last rain of late spring **nourishes the ten thousand things**, and **nourishes the human body** as well.

## I. The Three Pentads of Gǔyǔ

- **First pentad — Duckweed begins to grow (萍始生):** Floating duckweed starts to grow — **water-dampness gradually waxes**
- **Second pentad — Turtle doves preen their wings (鸣鸠拂其羽):** Turtle doves beat their wings — **yang qi (阳气, yángqì) rises**
- **Third pentad — The hoopoe alights on the mulberry (戴胜降于桑):** The hoopoe descends to the mulberry tree — **the work of silkworms is about to begin**

The three pentads show **water, wood and creatures all at play** —
the very emblem of **deep spring**.

## II. Three Characteristics of the Gǔyǔ Season

### 1. Rainfall Increases

At Gǔyǔ, in the **middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze**, "**Qīngmíng severs snow, Gǔyǔ severs frost**":
temperatures rise, **rains come often**.
South China says "**spring rain is as precious as oil**"; the north says "**spring rain moistens like cream**".

**When the rain is heavy, dampness is abundant**, and the Spleen (脾, pí) is most easily encumbered.

### 2. Temperatures Rise

By Gǔyǔ, **temperatures hold steady above 20 °C**.
The principle of "**bundle up in spring, freeze in autumn**" must be **gradually adjusted**,
but **do not strip off your clothes abruptly** — "**Gǔyǔ can kill the old mice with cold**."

### 3. Yang Qi Rises to Its Peak

Gǔyǔ marks the **end of the three months of spring**.
The body's **rising yang qi (阳气)** reaches **its highest point**,
and is about to transition into the **"growth" (长, zhǎng) stage of summer**.

## III. Three Keys to Health Preservation at Gǔyǔ

### 🌧 Key One: Dispel Dampness and Fortify the Spleen

> 「**湿困脾，谷雨湿盛，最宜健脾祛湿。**」 ("Dampness encumbers the Spleen; at Gǔyǔ when dampness is abundant, it is best to fortify the Spleen and dispel dampness.")

#### Dietary Damp-Dispelling

- ✅ **Yìmǐ (薏米, Job's tears / coix seed)** — fortifies the Spleen and percolates dampness
- ✅ **Chìxiǎodòu (赤小豆, adzuki / "small red bean")** — disinhibits water and reduces swelling
- ✅ **Shānyào (山药, Chinese yam)** — tonifies the Spleen and benefits the Kidneys
- ✅ **Winter melon (冬瓜, dōngguā)** — clears heat and disinhibits water
- ✅ **Crucian carp (鲫鱼, jìyú)** — fortifies the Spleen and disinhibits dampness

**Recommended:** **Yìmǐ-chìxiǎodòu congee (薏米赤小豆粥)** — the breakfast of choice for the Gǔyǔ season.

#### Damp-Dispelling Daily Routine

- ✅ **Keep the dwelling dry** — close windows on rainy days
- ✅ **Sun bedding and quilts** — sunshine transforms dampness
- ✅ **Exercise to sweat lightly** — a faint perspiration
- ❌ **Getting drenched in rain** — dampness enters the *còu lǐ* (腠理, interstices)
- ❌ **Sitting long** — Spleen transportation fails

#### Meridian Damp-Dispelling

- ✅ **Yīnlíngquán (阴陵泉, SP-9)** — the foremost point for fortifying the Spleen and dispelling dampness
- ✅ **Fēnglóng (丰隆, ST-40)** — transforms phlegm and dispels dampness
- ✅ **Zúsānlǐ (足三里, ST-36)** — fortifies the Spleen and harmonizes the Stomach

### 🌿 Key Two: Nourish the Liver and Free Coursing

Gǔyǔ is **late spring**; **Liver qi (肝气, gānqì) is still vigorous**,
but **already handing over to Heart fire (心火, xīnhuǒ)**.

> 「**春与肝相应。**」 ("Spring corresponds to the Liver.")

#### Liver-Coursing Tea Drinks

- **Rose flower tea:** 5 rose blossoms, steeped in 80 °C water
- **Chrysanthemum and cassia seed tea:** 5 chrysanthemums + 10 g jué míng zǐ (决明子, cassia seed)
- **Buddha's hand-tangerine peel drink:** 10 g fóshǒu (佛手) + 6 g chénpí (陈皮)

#### Liver-Coursing Meridians

- **Tàichōng (太冲, LV-3)** — courses the Liver and resolves depression
- **Qīmén (期门, LV-14)** — regulates Liver and Spleen
- **Tapping the Gallbladder Meridian (敲胆经)** — invigorates yang qi

### ☀ Key Three: At the Joint of Spring and Summer, Prevent «Rising Fire»

After Gǔyǔ, **summer is just around the corner**.
**Internal heat gradually arises** in the body,
and **Heart fire (心火) and Liver fire (肝火)** stir easily.

#### Preventing Rising Fire

- ✅ **Drink plenty of water** — 1500-2000 ml daily
- ✅ **Eat plain foods** — vegetables, fruits
- ✅ **Eat bitter flavors** — bitter melon, romaine lettuce, lotus seed core
- ✅ **Sleep early, rise early** — to follow yang qi
- ❌ **Less spicy** — chilies, strong liquor
- ❌ **Less greasy** — fried foods, fatty meat
- ❌ **Less temper** — restrain anger and course the Liver

## IV. The Seasonal Vegetable of Gǔyǔ — Chinese Toon

> 「**雨前香椿嫩如丝，雨后椿芽生木质。**」 ("Before the rain, Chinese toon is tender as silk; after the rain, the buds become woody.")

Around Gǔyǔ, **Chinese toon (香椿, xiāngchūn)** is at its tenderest —
the finest of «**vegetables of the trees**».

### Nutrition and Effects of Chinese Toon

- ✅ **Fortifies the Spleen and opens appetite** — its distinctive fragrance
- ✅ **Clears heat and resolves toxin** — cool in nature, bitter in flavor
- ✅ **Antibacterial and anti-inflammatory** — contains toonin
- ✅ **Anti-aging** — contains vitamin E
- ✅ **Dispels wind and disinhibits dampness** — the seasonal vegetable for Gǔyǔ's heavy damp

### Ways to Prepare Chinese Toon

1. **Toon scrambled with egg** — the most classic
2. **Toon with cold tofu** — the most refreshing
3. **Toon «fish»** — the most elegant
4. **Toon noodles** — the most home-style

### Cautions When Eating Chinese Toon

- ❌ **Chinese toon contains nitrites** — **blanch before eating** for safety
- ❌ **Those with yin deficiency and effulgent fire (阴虚火旺)** should eat less (**toon is cool**)
- ❌ **The chronically ill** should consume in **moderation** (**antioxidant but with renal toxicity**)

## V. Gǔyǔ Tea — «Gǔyǔ Tea»

> 「**谷雨谷雨，采茶对雨。**」 ("Gǔyǔ, Gǔyǔ — pick tea in the rain.")

Tea picked at the Gǔyǔ season is called «**Gǔyǔ tea**»,
also known as «**pre-rain tea**» (the tea harvested between Qīngmíng and Gǔyǔ).

### Characteristics of Gǔyǔ Tea

- **The last train of spring tea** — fine, tender buds and leaves
- **Rich in nutrients** — high in vitamins and amino acids
- **Clears fire and brightens the eyes** — seasonal clearing and nourishing
- **Lively flavor** — the best of spring tea

### Recommended Gǔyǔ Tea Drinks

- **Gǔyǔ green tea** — clears heat and brightens the eyes
- **Gǔyǔ black tea** — warms and nourishes the Stomach
- **Gǔyǔ white tea** — antioxidant, protects the Liver

### Cautions for Gǔyǔ Tea

- ❌ **Not on an empty stomach** — irritates the Stomach
- ❌ **Not before sleep** — affects sleep
- ❌ **Not cold tea** — injures the Spleen and Stomach
- ❌ **Not strong tea** — injures the Heart and Kidneys
- ✅ **Warm, light tea** nourishes most

## VI. Gǔyǔ Exercise — «Brisk Walking»

> 「**谷雨三朝看牡丹。**」 ("Three mornings of Gǔyǔ — go to see the peonies.")

Around Gǔyǔ, **peonies (牡丹, mǔdān)** are in full bloom —
an **excellent time** for **outdoor exercise**.

### Recommended Exercise

- ✅ **Brisk walking** — 30-60 minutes daily
- ✅ **Mountain hiking** — view flowers and gaze far, course the Liver and fortify the Spleen
- ✅ **Tai Chi (太极拳)** — harmonizes qi and blood
- ✅ **Kite-flying** — looking up to the distance, **nourishes the eyes and protects the Liver**
- ✅ **Flower viewing** — cultivates emotion, resolves constraint

### Cautions for Exercise

- ❌ **Profuse sweating** — "**sweat is the fluid of the Heart; excessive sweating injures yang (汗为心液，过汗伤阳)**"
- ❌ **Strenuous activity** — at a seasonal joint, **be gentle, not abrupt**
- ❌ **Empty stomach** — exercise **1 hour after meals**
- ✅ **Stop at a faint sweat**

## VII. Gǔyǔ Emotions — «Harmony Above All»

> 「**谷雨时节，情志宜舒。**」 ("In the Gǔyǔ season, emotion should be at ease.")

Gǔyǔ is **the end of spring**.
**The rise of Liver qi is near its peak**;
**emotions** are most prone to **swing**.

#### Restraining the Emotions

- ✅ **Maintain a pleasant mood** — music, reading
- ✅ **Steer clear of disputes** — do not quarrel
- ✅ **Get close to nature** — see flowers, hike
- ✅ **Restrain anger** — great anger injures the Liver

#### Preventing Depression

- ✅ **Get more sun** — sunlight suppresses melatonin
- ✅ **Exercise more** — release endorphins
- ✅ **Communicate more** — confiding eases pressure
- ✅ **Sleep more** — *zi-wǔ* naps (子午觉) nourish best

## Conclusion

> 「**春雨贵如油，养生正当时。**」 ("Spring rain is as precious as oil; it is the very moment for nourishing life.")

Gǔyǔ is **spring's last poem**,
the **handover between spring and summer**,
the **opening of yang qi at its highest peak**.

Seize this **golden window of late-spring damp-dispelling** —

- Fortify the Spleen and **dispel dampness**;
- Nourish the Liver and **course freely**;
- Clear heat and **prevent rising fire**;
- Outdoors, **brisk-walk**;
- Listening to rain, **savor the fragrance of tea**.

Let these **spring rains**
**soak through** the **whole body's vitality**,
preparing us for the «**growth**» of summer.

**After Gǔyǔ comes Lìxià (立夏, Beginning of Summer)**.
**The full effort of spring** finds its **close at Gǔyǔ**.
